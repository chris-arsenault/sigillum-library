# Reinforce / Contrast Comping: a comp's interaction-stance toward a lead (survey, NOT distilled)

The technique: an accompaniment (the strongest case: a **PIANO COMP under a WIND or VOICE LEAD**) that
**toggles its STANCE toward the lead's PLAYED notes** — between **REINFORCING** (hitting/doubling WITH
the lead: rhythmic unison on its accents, harmonic agreement, a shared stab — "playing WITH the
soloist") and **CONTRASTING** (answering in the lead's rests, moving against a held note, contrary
rhythm/motion, a surprising harmony — "playing AROUND/AGAINST the soloist"). The *alternation* is the
life of it: pure reinforce = dead doubling, pure contrast = the lock is lost. This is the comp's
**interaction stance**, and it is a distinct axis from the complementary-rhythm survey (answer-in-the-gap / hocket /
complementary rhythm). The complementary-rhythm survey asks *"is the composite continuous?"*; THIS asks *"at each event, is
the comp WITH the lead or AGAINST it — and does it switch?"* The two interlock (contrast-mode usually
IS gap-filling) but reinforce-mode is the complementary-rhythm survey's blind spot: the comp deliberately doubling the lead's
attack is the opposite of complementary rhythm, and is half of this technique. Card: **D9_REINFORCE_
CONTRAST** is the hand-composed prior; this annex is its research ground and the source for a new card.
Pitches scientific, middle C = C4.

## 1. The names (overlapping but DISTINCT — they differ on WHICH UNIT the stance attaches to, and on idiom)

The single best-named pair for the whole idea comes from **Hal Galper** (Phil Woods' / Cannonball's
pianist), who calls the two stances **CLOSE COMPING** and **INDEPENDENT COMPING**:

- **CLOSE COMPING ( = REINFORCE / "WITH").** Galper: *"'Close comping' is when you respond to the
  soloist quickly and 'answer' a musical idea."* You match and catch the soloist's phrasing — land on
  their accents, echo their rhythm, agree harmonically. His maxim: **"all comping ideas come from the
  soloist!"** Phil Woods *preferred* this (a comper who locks to him). The reinforce extreme is
  literally hitting the same attack the lead hits — a shared accent.
- **INDEPENDENT COMPING ( = CONTRAST / "AGAINST/AROUND").** You intentionally stay separate, supplying
  contrast rather than reinforcement; *"the soloist plays against your comping rather than being
  supported by it."* Joe Henderson and James Moody *preferred* this.
- **Galper's diagnostic (the toggle is real and players have a preference):** if the soloist reacts
  negatively when you MATCH their ideas, they want independent comping; if no reaction, close comping
  works. So a real comper is *reading* the lead in real time and choosing a stance — which is exactly
  the toggle this card composes-out.
- Galper's craft notes on the contrast side: *"Not every idea a soloist plays needs a response"*;
  create **"the illusion of space"** through restraint; **increase activity at turnarounds and when the
  soloist leaves longer gaps**; vary **"chords of different duration and volume, activity and
  inactivity."** (So contrast-mode clusters in the lead's gaps; reinforce-mode clusters on the lead's
  active accents — the inverse of the complementary-rhythm survey's complementary-value-classes ONLY in reinforce-mode.)

Idiom-specific names for the SAME two stances:

- **BIG-BAND FIGURE TYPES (the arranger's pair).** A written-out version of the same toggle, attaching
  the stance to *figures*, not improvised events:
  - **MECHANICAL / SECTIONAL / "concerted" figure ( = REINFORCE):** the accompaniment is rhythmically
    LOCKED to the lead line (the section moves in the melody's rhythm; "block/concerted" writing). When
    the rhythm section "**catches**" the figure — hits the same accents the horns hit — that's reinforce
    at the band level. Drummer's term: **"kicking the band"** / **"kicks"** (aggressively, *kicks*;
    a rim-shot snare accent specifically = a **"punch"**), and **"setting up"** a hit (a fill/cue that
    leads the band INTO the shared accent so they land together). Galper-style "close comping" is the
    small-group version of catching figures.
  - **BACKGROUND FIGURE ( = CONTRAST):** *"Background figures nearly always fill the gaps in and around
    the melody, usually with a DIFFERENT section of instruments, and great care should be taken if the
    stabs or backing figures are not in the gaps of the tune, as they may confuse the melody."* This is
    the contrast/answer stance, with the explicit warning that contrast must *avoid* clashing with the
    lead's onsets — i.e. land in the holes. (taming-the-saxophone / scoredchanges ensemble-writing.)
  - Backing flavours within contrast: **sustained pad** (long chords behind the line — neutral floor),
    **rhythmic stabs** (short answers in the gaps), **answering fill/countermelody** (a little melody in
    the breath). "The melody should obviously predominate over the backings."
- **ANTICIPATION / "the push" (the micro-timing edge of reinforce).** Standard jazz-arranging rule:
  *"a syncopated note anticipating a beat by an eighth note or less is usually harmonised with the
  chord of the beat FOLLOWING the anticipation"* — the comp arrives on the next chord's tones an eighth
  EARLY (the "and-of-4" into the downbeat), which **"creates a sense of forward motion, over-the-bar-
  line phrasing, and excitement"** (Jazzadvice, *harmonic anticipation*). Crucial coordination rule:
  **"do not combine an anticipation in one instrument/section with an on-beat note in another"** — if
  the comp pushes, the lead pushes WITH it (reinforce includes matching the *displacement*, not just
  the pitch). A pushed shared accent is the most energized form of reinforce.
- **GOSPEL "PREACHING CHORDS" / call-and-response vamp (the speech-idiom version).** Hammond/piano chords
  *"to accentuate, emphasize, and RESPOND to a pastor's sermon"* — improvised to *"imitate the voices
  of the preachers and the calls-and-responses of the congregations."* The two stances are vivid here:
  **punctuating the words** (a chord ON the cadence of a spoken/sung phrase = reinforce) vs **answering
  in the breath** (a fill in the gap between phrases = contrast). Gospel/R&B accompaniment of a singer:
  *"fill in the gaps with fills and embellishments while the singer is resting or while the melody is
  static"* (contrast), against landing a stab on the downbeat of the hook WITH the singer (reinforce).
- **LATIN MONTUNO / GUAJEO / TUMBAO (the ostinato version — locked to a third party, the clave).** The
  piano *montuno* (older name *guajeo*; the bass version is the *tumbao*) is a repeating riff whose
  attacks are *fixed against the CLAVE*, not the melody. So in salsa the "reinforce vs contrast"
  reference is the **clave**, not the lead: the montuno locks WITH the clave (reinforce the groove) while
  often sitting in CONTRAST to the sung melody. Interlocking guajeos (piano / tres / trumpets each a
  cell) tile a composite — relevant as the *fixed-against-a-grid* extreme of reinforce, where the comp
  is rigidly WITH the timeline and the lead floats over it.
- **CONCERTANTE / OBBLIGATO ACCOMPANIMENT (the classical ancestor).** Mozart's wind-and-piano dialogue
  (Quintet K.452; concerti Nos. 18, 19) is *obbligato* accompaniment — the piano *"revisits themes and
  embellishes them, turning the movement into a conversation,"* the orchestra *"coming to the fore now
  and then but content to offer accompaniment."* The classical pair: the accompaniment either **doubles/
  shadows the soloist** (reinforce — *colla parte*, doubling at the 3rd/6th/octave) or supplies an
  **independent counterline / answers in the soloist's rests** (contrast — a true second voice). The
  alternation of these IS the classical "dialogue."

**Which name for THIS card?** The behaviour is **the toggle between Galper's close (reinforce) and
independent (contrast) comping**, scored as the arranger's **concerted/mechanical figure** (reinforce)
vs **background figure in the gaps** (contrast), with **anticipation/pushes** as the energized reinforce
flavour. The wind/voice-lead-over-piano-comp realization (the user's stated strongest version) is the
*obbligato/concertante* texture done with jazz comping vocabulary.

## 2. The WHY (why the toggle, specifically, is alive — and why each pole alone is dead)

- **Reinforce = ACCENT FUSION + CONFIRMATION.** When two different-timbre voices attack the SAME instant
  with agreeing pitch, the listener fuses them into one louder, "confirmed" event (a shared accent reads
  as emphasis/arrival; the comp *seconds* the lead's gesture). It says "this note matters; I'm with
  you." But sustained reinforce = **doubling = dead**: if the comp only ever lands where the lead lands,
  it adds nothing the lead didn't already say, and the second timbre is wasted. (This is exactly the
  failure the complementary-rhythm survey §4g warns of: *"pure reinforcement is doubling (dead)."*)
- **Contrast = MOTION + CONVERSATION + RELIEF.** When the comp moves where the lead is still (fills a
  rest, moves against a held note, answers a phrase), it (1) keeps the composite surface moving (the complementary-rhythm survey), (2) reads as a *second character* talking back (call-and-response), (3) gives the lead's line
  RELIEF — the ear refocuses on the comp, then re-hears the lead. But sustained contrast = **the lock is
  lost**: if the comp never agrees with the lead, the two layers feel like two unrelated pieces; there's
  no "we" — no shared downbeat the listener can hang the meter on.
- **The toggle is the music.** Alternating gives you BOTH: periodic shared accents (a "we" / arrival
  points / meter anchors) AND periodic conversation (motion / second voice / relief). The *placement of
  the switch* is expressive: reinforce a phrase's PEAK or its CADENCE (agree on the important note);
  contrast through the phrase's BODY and in its BREATHS (talk back while it rests). Galper's "increase
  activity at turnarounds / when the soloist leaves gaps" is precisely: **be in contrast-mode (active) in
  the gaps, reinforce-mode (locked) on the structural arrivals.** A *surprise* is to reinforce where the
  ear expected contrast (suddenly the comp slams the lead's note WITH it) or to contrast a note the ear
  expected reinforced (the comp ducks the cadence and answers instead).
- **Anticipation is the hottest reinforce.** A reinforce that's PUSHED (lead + comp both an eighth early
  onto the next chord) doesn't just confirm — it *propels*. The rule "don't push in one part against an
  on-beat in another" means the energized reinforce demands the comp catch the lead's displacement, not
  just its pitch — so reinforce-with-push is a tighter lock than reinforce-on-the-beat.

## 3. Concrete note-level examples (constructed to the rules above; G minor, 4/4, q=120 for portability)

Use these as the technique's vocabulary; the card draws from them.

### 3a. REINFORCE on a shared accent (close comping; concerted figure)
Lead (clarinet) hits a syncopated accent on the "and-of-2": `... D5(q on b1) G5(8th)–(rest 8th)–B-5(8th
on "and-of-2") ...`. Comp lands the SAME instant with an agreeing voicing: `[G3,B-3,D4] struck on the
"and-of-2"`, same eighth. Two timbres, one attack → fused accent. This is "catching the figure."

### 3b. REINFORCE with a PUSH (anticipation) into a downbeat
Chord changes to Cm on the next downbeat (b3). Lead anticipates: `... A5(8th)–C6 on "and-of-2"(tied over
b3)`; the comp arrives on the Cm voicing `[C4,E-4,G4]` on the SAME "and-of-2", harmonised with the
chord of the FOLLOWING beat (b3) — both push together; nothing sits on-beat b3 against the push. Forward
drive.

### 3c. CONTRAST in the gap (background stab / answer-in-the-breath)
Lead phrase ends, rests on b3: `... F5(q) D5(q) [REST half] ...`. Comp answers the silence with a short
contrary stab a register apart: `[B-3,D4,F4](8th) on b3, then (rest)` — lands in the hole, doesn't
clash with any lead onset (the §1 background-figure rule). Optionally a 2-note fill `E-4–D4` walking
into the lead's re-entry (the fill anticipates the lead's next note = double duty).
**Contrast HARMONY surprise:** over a sustained lead `G5(half)` the comp moves to a borrowed/coloured
chord underneath (`[E-3,G3,C#4]` — a Ger+6 / tritone colour) and resolves as the lead re-attacks — the
comp moved AGAINST the held note with a surprising harmony.

### 3d. CONTRAST by contrary motion against a held note
Lead holds `D5` (half). Comp inner voice walks DOWN against it (`B-3 → A3 → G3` in quarters) — contrary
to the lead's stasis, a moving second voice. Reads as conversation, not doubling.

### 3e. The TOGGLE in one phrase (the whole technique in 2 bars)
Bar A (lead active, ascending 8ths `D5 E-5 F5 G5 | A5 B-5`): comp **reinforces** — locks to the lead's
8th rhythm in agreeing 3rds/6ths under it (concerted), landing the bar's peak `B-5` WITH the lead as a
full `[B-3,D4,G4,B-5-area]` accent. Bar B (lead rests / holds): comp flips to **contrast** — fills the
breath with an off-beat answering stab and a contrary inner line, the lead's held note un-doubled. The
listener hears "with you … now talking back."

## 4. Scoring / craft notes specific to this technique

- **Timbre contrast is mandatory** (the complementary-rhythm survey §4g): lead = wind/voice (clarinet/oboe/flute/sung), comp =
  piano. Different colour means even in reinforce-mode the two never *fully* fuse — the lead stays in
  relief — so reinforce reads as "doubled accent," not "thickened unison." (A piano-only realization of
  this card is the weak case; the colour split does what register/dynamics do in solo piano.)
- **A third value-class anchors the toggle.** A steady bass (LH walking quarters, or a tumbao) under the
  RH's switching stance gives the ear a constant timeline to measure "with vs against" against — and lets
  the RH be sparse without the texture dropping out. (Mirrors the complementary-rhythm survey §5.3 complementary value-classes;
  D9 already does this with a quarter walk.)
- **Voicing for reinforce vs contrast differ.** Reinforce voicings AGREE with the lead's pitch (the
  lead's note is IN or consonant-with the comp chord — guide-tones/3rds-6ths under the lead, the piano-voicing survey §3,
  §5). Contrast voicings can be rootless/coloured/away from the lead (the piano-voicing survey §2) and can introduce the
  surprising harmony. Keep the bass off low close 3rds/4ths (the piano-voicing survey §1 LIL).
- **Mark the stance.** Like D9, label each event-group `txt:REINFORCE` / `txt:CONTRAST` so the card
  TEACHES the toggle (it's a library reference). Match-the-displacement on pushes.
- **Placement rule (the expressive core):** reinforce the structural notes (phrase PEAK, the CADENCE);
  contrast the body and the breaths. Save one *surprise* switch (reinforce-where-contrast-expected or
  vice-versa) for a single high point. Don't toggle every bar mechanically — the switch is a gesture.

## 5. How this differs from / complements the prior card D9 and the complementary-rhythm survey

- **vs the complementary-rhythm survey (gap/hocket/complementary rhythm):** it is about the *composite* (continuity, the union
  of attacks fills the rests). This card is about the comp's *stance* (with vs against), of which
  contrast-mode overlaps it but reinforce-mode is its inverse (deliberate doubling of the lead's attack).
  The named axis here is **close vs independent comping** (Galper), not hocket.
- **vs D9 (the hand-composed prior):** D9 is excellent and already alternates reinforce/contrast over a
  walking bass with labelled stances. Its weighting leans on the *contrast* side (16th/off-beat stabs,
  hocket-y gaps — it grew out of the complementary-rhythm survey). A research-led card can (a) make REINFORCE more vivid — true
  shared accents and at least one **anticipation/push** where lead+comp land the SAME pushed eighth
  (D9's reinforce is mostly on-beat 8ths *under* the lead, less a slammed shared accent); (b) include a
  **contrast-by-surprising-harmony** moment (comp moving against a HELD lead note, §3c/§3d — D9's
  contrast is mostly rhythmic gap-filling, less a harmonic move against a sustain); (c) stage one
  **surprise switch** at the high point. The new card can complement D9 by foregrounding the *reinforce*
  pole and the *harmonic* contrast that D9 underplays.
