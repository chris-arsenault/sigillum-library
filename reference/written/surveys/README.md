# surveys/ — technique surveys (research-led, source-cited)

Focused surveys of a single technique or problem, each with exemplars + sources. Not ordered — grouped
by what they cover. Cards and procedures cite them by name.

**Melody & harmonic implication**
- `implied_harmony_single_line.md`, `melodic_harmonic_implication_theory.md` — how a single line implies harmony.
- `harmonic_ambiguity_failure_modes.md`, `harmonic_mislead.md` — when implication fails / is bent deliberately.
- `descending_fifths_melodic_sequence.md`, `modal_melody.md`, `modulating_melody.md`,
  `jazz_outlining_changes.md`, `axis_recolor_engine.md` — sequence, mode, modulation, outlining changes, recolor.

**Texture, counterpoint & figuration**
- `fugue_construction.md` — fugue as a form: subject design, tonal/real answer, countersubject
  and invertible counterpoint, episodes, middle-section key schemes, stretto, final section,
  double fugue, gigue-fugue dialect; the `dsl:fugue` cards cite it by `fugue_construction:<section>`.
- `etude_pitch_devices.md` — the seven motif/pitch devices from the Selected Studies etudes
  (neighbor integrity, planted leading tones, widening returns, fixed points, arpeggio
  subversions, cadence-tail seeds, signature dissonance), bar-cited.
- `etude_figure_grammar.md` — how the same etudes avoid noodling and block-writing at
  figure/phrase level (spine bars, elided seams, vectored plateaus); measured with the
  sounding projections.
- `running_counterpoint.md`, `sixteenth_deployment.md` — moving inner lines / 16th deployment.
- `complementary_rhythm.md`, `reinforce_contrast.md` — composite-rhythm continuity vs. lead reinforcement/contrast.
- `ground_bass.md` — ground-bass / passacaglia as a cumulative engine.

**Orchestration**
- `orchestration_techniques.md` — the orchestration survey, covering every family (winds, brass,
  tutti, accompaniment, antiphony, doublings, color, chord_voicing, percussion_choir); the
  orchestration cards cite it by `orchestration_techniques:<family>`.
- `string_techniques.md`, `piano_voicing.md` — per-instrument-family craft.
