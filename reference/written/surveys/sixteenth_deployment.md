# Sixteenths IN CONTEXT: the singing melody over a flowing inner current (survey, NOT distilled)

The technique: **16th notes deployed as ONE STRATUM inside a mixed-rhythm texture — never the whole
fabric.** A melody breathes in LONG values (halves, dotted-quarter + 8th pickups, rests, space — it
SINGS) while a flowing, varied, heavily CHROMATIC 16th-note under-current runs beneath it in the OTHER
hand/voice. The two strata are rhythmically COMPLEMENTARY: the melody holds while the current moves;
the 16ths give motion / harmonic travel / colour, but they are the texture UNDER the subject, not the
subject. This is the real compositional skill the library was missing — the existing pure-16th cards
(K6_CADENZA_BURST, P8_CASCADE_ALTERNATE, F6_TOCCATA, the experiments RUNNING_LINE_MOTO /
RUNNING_COUNTERPOINT) teach 16ths as a MOTOR (the whole surface is 16ths, an étude); they do NOT teach
16ths AS ACCOMPANIMENT under a held melody. That is the gap this card fills.

Model studied: a grand-staff Romantic piano excerpt, F major / D minor, common time (the `a.png`
reference) — RH melody in halves / dotted-quarter+8th, whole bars of rest before the melody enters;
LH a chromatic varied 16th current (16th groups + 8ths + TIES across beats + dotted figures, saturated
with sharps/naturals/flats marking passing & neighbor motion, ranging an octave-plus per bar).
Pitches scientific, middle C = C4. Card produced: **P20_SINGING_OVER_CURRENT** (+ optional flourish
variant). Nearest existing cards: K1_NOCTURNE_34 (but its LH is 8th-note BROKEN-CHORD sweeps, not a
chromatic 16th current); F3_SCALAR_CURRENT (16th scalar, but as FOREGROUND not under a long melody);
O5_INNER_AGITATION / keyboard_figuration s5 (inner voices move under held outer voices, but orchestral & in 8ths).

## 1. The names (overlapping, but they differ on WHAT the 16ths are doing)

- **MELODY-AND-ACCOMPANIMENT / cantabile texture (the nocturne default)** — the umbrella. A melody
  "beautiful like a song" over an accompaniment "presented with arpeggios" at slow-to-medium tempo
  (the inherited Field/Chopin nocturne). The point that matters for THIS card: in a CONCERT nocturne
  the LH "undertakes more than merely rolling chords — it introduces counter-melodies, harmonic pedal
  points or delicate inner voices" (Taruskin; SE22). The accompaniment is a real moving stratum, not a
  chord-spelling grid. When that accompaniment runs in 16ths it becomes the "murmuring / flowing
  current" below.
- **MURMURING current / *mormorando* / "the clear stream"** — the specific name for a soft,
  continuous, fast inner figuration evoking flowing water. Burgmüller "La source / The Clear Stream"
  (*La limpide ruisseau*): "limpidly beautiful phrases from unravelled broken chords… a *mormorando*
  (murmuring effect)." The current MURMURS — it is dynamically recessive (one tier under the melody),
  even, continuous; its job is motion-as-atmosphere, not statement. (Arioso7/Kirsten on Burgmüller.)
- **FIORITURA / flourish** ("flowering") — "lengthy, complex embellishments, as opposed to standardized
  local ornamental figures such as trills, mordents, appoggiaturas" (Wikipedia, citing Chopin's
  Nocturne in D♭). This is the 16ths-as-FLOURISH use: a florid run of 16ths/32nds that EMBELLISHES a
  melody note or connects two structural melody tones — it lives in the MELODY voice, momentarily, then
  the melody returns to long values. Distinct from the murmuring current (which lives in the
  accompaniment continuously). Related: **fioritura as diminution/division** (the 16th-century practice
  of dividing a long note into many short ones) — the historical root of "fill the long note with a run."
- **ANACRUSIS / pickup of 16ths** — "a note or notes leading into the downbeat… not a complete
  measure." Chopin's Op.9/2 begins with exactly this. The 16th-note USE here: a 16th flourish/run as an
  ANACRUSIS that LAUNCHES the next structural note — the burst lands ON the downbeat, then the melody
  holds. (HelloMusicTheory; Wikipedia/Anacrusis; Op.9/2.)
- **INNER-VOICE AGITATION (Brahms inwardness)** — when the 16ths/8ths live in a MIDDLE voice between a
  held top melody and a slow bass, "concealing melodies and bass in inner voices gives Brahms's piano
  music its characteristic inward quality" (Cubero, *Inwardness and Inner Melodies in Brahms's Piano
  Works*, MTO 23.1). The structural melody can itself hide in the middle; the moving 16ths animate the
  texture from inside. "Inner voices MOVE while outer voices hold long notes" (keyboard_figuration s5).
- **AGITATO / doppio-movimento INTENSIFICATION (the intensity dial)** — the 16th current as a DIAL, not
  a constant. Chopin Op.48/1: the return of the opening melody "is accompanied by much richer
  figuration and is marked *agitato*"; "a rising chromatic figure introduces a new rhythmic stream of
  16ths… until the rambunctious stream eventually takes over." Op.9/3 recap "developed not by
  intensified ornamentation but by a complete reworking of the SUPPORTING TEXTURE over which the
  principal melody appears almost completely unaltered." The melody stays put; the CURRENT escalates
  (8ths → 16ths → both-hands 16ths) then settles. (Taruskin Challenge; Wikipedia Op.48, Op.55.)

## 2. The mechanics — WHY each piece works (note-level)

### 2a. The melody must be LONG-VALUE and breathe (else it's an étude, not a piece)
- The subject is carried in halves, dotted-quarters + 8th continuations, whole-bar holds, and RESTS.
  Op.9/2 (E♭): the tune is dotted-8th+16th and 8th figures over a wide swinging LH, but its STRUCTURAL
  motion is slow — long sustained tops, ornamental 16ths only as fioritura decoration ON those tops.
- Aerith's-theme logic (melody_primacy §3): "a strong 4-bar melody… melody is the structure, not decoration over
  a progression"; leaps RATIONED to the peak. The current never competes in the melody's octave (R8
  foreground law): "tune on top," accompaniment one tier down, the melody's register cleared.
- The melody's RESTS are load-bearing. They are where the current is briefly audible alone (the
  murmur surfaces), or where a FILL erupts (§2d).

### 2b. The current must be VARIED — 16th-DRIVEN, not 100% straight 16ths
This is the single rule that separates a composed current from a motoric étude. The flowing
accompaniment is *predominantly* 16ths but it BREATHES via:
- **TIES across the beat / barline** — a 16th or 8th tied over so the stream isn't metronomically
  re-articulated every beat (visible in `a.png`: ties bind a 16th into the next group). The tie is what
  makes the current "flow" rather than "tick."
- **mixed values inside the run** — 8ths and dotted-8th+16th figures interleaved with the 16th groups
  (keyboard_figuration s6d: "a phrase traverses ≥2-3 value classes inside itself"). Burgmüller and the nocturne LH
  alternate 16th sextiplet-feel groups with 8th connectors.
- **the figure VARIES per bar** (keyboard_figuration s4 "figuration variation law"): crest shape, span, register, the
  chromatic spelling all change phrase to phrase while the identity (a flowing chromatic current) holds.
  "The same chord NEVER voiced identically in consecutive phrases."

### 2c. The current must be CHROMATIC and VOICE-LED (the colour)
- Saturated with accidentals: **chromatic PASSING tones** (filling a whole step with a 16th between two
  chord tones) and **chromatic NEIGHBOR tones** (a 16th half-step above/below a held tone, returning).
  `a.png`'s LH is dense with sharps/naturals/flats doing exactly this — the chromaticism lives in the
  16th-speed connective tissue, the harmony underneath staying simple.
- This is **diminution with chromatic infill**: the current connects the chord tones of a slow
  progression by the shortest chromatic path, so each 16th is voice-led (a step, usually a half-step)
  from its neighbour. The harmony travels smoothly BECAUSE the current chromatically threads it.
- Brahms (Op.116/6) "descending motions countered by rising chromatic lines in the inner voice";
  (Op.116/5) "a chromatic neighbor figure around the Kopfton." The chromatic move is a real voice, not
  a colour-wash.

### 2d. The three deployment USES of 16ths (the whole point — 16ths as a DEVICE)
1. **CURRENT (continuous under-current)** — the `a.png` default: the 16ths run continuously in the
   accompaniment hand, the melody rides on top in long values. Murmuring stream / nocturne LH /
   Brahms inner agitation. The card's primary texture.
2. **FILL (in the melody's rests)** — when the melody rests, a 16th run surfaces INTO the gap and
   recedes when the melody re-enters (complementary rhythm, see `complementary_rhythm.md`; keyboard_figuration s6:
   "melody long note ⇒ the other hand moves"). The fill can be in the accompaniment OR a brief
   fioritura answering the melody's own phrase-end.
3. **FLOURISH / ANACRUSIS / BURST** — a 16th (or 32nd) run as an embellishment that LAUNCHES a
   structural note (anacrusis landing on the downbeat), or DECORATES a held melody tone (fioritura —
   fill the long note with division), or ERUPTS as an intensification then SETTLES back to long values
   (the agitato dial). The burst is RATIONED — it is an event, like a saved leap; if it's everywhere
   it's an étude again. "Pickups of 16ths launch the next chord" (keyboard_figuration s6d).

### 2e. Complementarity — the union-surface law (keyboard_figuration s6)
- "Union of all layers' attacks ≈ a continuous surface rhythm in every non-void window — WHILE no
  single layer plays it all." For two hands: melody long note ⇒ the OTHER hand moves. The current
  carries the surface while the melody holds; when the melody finally MOVES (a run, a cadential
  flourish), the current can THIN (longer values, or rest) so they never both saturate. The strata
  trade the motion-duty. This is the cheapest test that separates composed accompaniment from token
  accompaniment.

## 3. Repertoire (concrete, across the idiom)
- **Chopin Nocturne Op.9/2 (E♭)** — anacrusis opening; melody in dotted-8th+16th over a wide-swing LH;
  fioritura 16th/32nd runs decorating sustained tops (the flourish-on-a-held-note use).
- **Chopin Nocturne Op.48/1 (c)** — the *agitato* return: melody unaltered, the SUPPORTING texture
  escalates into 16th streams (the intensity dial; two opposed streams, 16ths vs triplet 16ths).
- **Chopin Nocturne Op.9/3, Op.55** — recap by "reworking the supporting texture," melody almost
  unchanged over it.
- **Brahms Intermezzi Op.116/5, Op.116/6, Op.118/2** — held outer melody, chromatic moving INNER voice;
  structural melody concealed in the middle; "inner voices move while outer voices hold."
- **Liszt Consolation No.3 (D♭)** — nocturne-style: singing top note over a low pedal D♭ and a flowing
  broken-chord LH current; opening "recalls Chopin's Nocturne Op.27/2."
- **Fauré nocturnes/barcarolles** — long-breathed melody over a rocking flowing accompaniment (the
  barcarolle 6/8 current; the duple-melody-over-flowing-LH nocturne texture).
- **Rachmaninoff Prelude in E♭ (Op.23/6)** — "16th-note accompaniment expands into two hands, rising
  upward in a CHROMATIC pattern" under a sung melody (the chromatic current saturating).
- **Burgmüller "La source / The Clear Stream"** — the pedagogical model of the *mormorando* murmuring
  16th current from unravelled broken chords; melody floats over it.
- **Field nocturnes** — the original "clear melody-plus-arpeggiated-accompaniment" Chopin broadened.

## 4. Note-level example sketches (for the card writer)
- **Murmuring chromatic current (F major / D minor area), LH, one bar of 16ths with a tie and a
  chromatic passing tone:** F2 A2 C3 E3 — F3(tie)—F3 E3 D#3 E3 — G3 F3 E3 D3 — C3 B2 C3 (8th) … the
  D#3 is a chromatic LOWER neighbor to E3; the run never re-articulates the beat where the tie sits.
- **Fill in a melody rest:** melody holds a half note then RESTS a beat; in the rest the current
  briefly RISES (C4 D4 E♭4 E♮4 F4 G4 — chromatic E♭→E♮ passing) cresting just as the melody re-enters
  on the downbeat above it, then the current sinks back under.
- **Flourish/anacrusis into a structural note:** last 16ths of a bar = G4 A4 B♭4 B♮4 (chromatic
  approach) → C5 (the melody's structural arrival, a half note) ON the downbeat. The run is the
  upbeat to the held note.
- **Intensity burst then settle:** two bars of even murmuring current → one bar where the current
  thickens to a both-hands chromatic 16th surge (the agitato eruption) → the melody holds its
  highest note → the current SETTLES back to single-hand murmur for the cadence.

## 5. What this is NOT (the étude trap to avoid)
- NOT a moto-perpetuo line (RUNNING_LINE_MOTO) — there the 16ths ARE the melody/whole fabric.
- NOT a running two-voice invention (RUNNING_COUNTERPOINT) — there both voices are equal 16th lines.
- NOT a cadenza/cascade (K6, P8) — there the 16ths are a pure-virtuosic gesture with no held melody.
- The proof a card escapes the trap: (a) the melody voice is PREDOMINANTLY ≥ quarter-note values;
  (b) the accompaniment is 16th-DRIVEN but VARIED (contains ties / 8ths / mixed values — not 100%
  straight 16ths) and CHROMATIC (accidentals); (c) the two strata are rhythmically COMPLEMENTARY
  (melody holds while accompaniment moves; the union is a continuous surface neither plays alone).
