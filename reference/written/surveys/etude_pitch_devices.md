# Pitch and Interval: The Motif Device Survey (Selected Studies)

Bar-by-bar pitch/interval analysis of the three Selected Studies clarinet etudes —
what each motif IS intervalically, how every return transforms it, and what the
accidentals are doing. Written pitch (the score the player reads); every claim
verified against extracted note tables. The seven recurring devices are catalogued at
the end; cards cite them as `etude_pitch_devices:device N`. Per-piece reviews:
`/home/dev/repos/etudes/reviews/`.

## Wiedemann: the neighbor-turn law and the arpeggio subversions

**The motif (b1):** two clauses of fixed interval content. Clause 1: semitone
lower-neighbor turn (F#4-E#4-F#4). Clause 2: rise a third (A4), neighbor down (G#4), leap
a FOURTH up (C#5). The bar spells the tonic triad, every chord tone approached by
neighbor, capped by the rising fourth.

**b1-5, the turn walked up the triad with chromatic enforcement:**
- b2 = b1 exact (the statement earns its recognition).
- b3: the whole double-clause UP A FOURTH onto B: B-A#-B | D-C#-F#. The diatonic lower
  neighbor of B is A-natural (a whole tone) - the piece writes **A# to preserve the
  turn's semitone identity**. First law: accidentals exist to keep the motif's interval
  content invariant at new stations.
- b4: turn on D (C#-D is already a semitone - no accidental needed), clause 2 INVERTED
  downward: D5 drops a diminished 4th to A#4, resolves up to B4, falls a 4th to F#4 -
  closing the four-bar arch back to the tonic register (F#4 ... F#5 peak at b3's end ...
  F#4).
- b5: the law at its extreme - the turn lands ON THE LEADING TONE E#, so its semitone
  neighbor must be **D-double-sharp**. Then clause 2 collapses into the dominant: G#4
  falls a tritone to **D-natural** (the b9 of C#7) then C#4. The bar is V7b9 spelled as a
  line: E# resolves up by semitone, D-natural resolves down by semitone - the dominant's
  two tension notes closing in contrary motion. The "deeper accidentals" are all
  functional: LT, its enforced neighbor, and the b9.
- b6-7: the discrete clauses dissolve into 6-note slurred winding built from the SAME
  interval cells (semitone neighbor, fourth-leap) without the turn's symmetry: A4-E#4
  (dim 4th down, the LT resolution embedded mid-stream)-F#4-C#4-D4-B3, then the tonic
  arpeggio rise C#4-F#4-A4 with the E#4 neighbor closing into b8's cadence.
- b8 cadence + tail A4-G#4-F#4 (diatonic falling third: 3-2-1). Keep this tail in mind.

**b9-12, the ascent mechanism:** the "up-down-up" arch = rising 3-step foot + accented
peak + 2-step fall. The foot climbs stepwise per bar (E#-F#-G# / F#-G#-A / G#-A-B - note
it STARTS from the leading tone, i.e. b8's tail inverted); the accented peaks climb
B4-D5-F#5 (arpeggiating iv's triad); and the LEAP to each accent widens: G#-B = minor
3rd, A-D = 4th, B-F# = 5TH. Written-in intensification by interval growth. b12 runs the
foot twice - C#-D-E, then C#-D#-E# with 6 and 7 RAISED: the melodic-minor lift that makes
b13's octave return (13-18 = 1-6 note-for-note, 8va) arrive as a resolution, not a
restatement. The return's only new event is its exit: b19 telescopes b4's inverted clause
into a two-octave tonic-arpeggio plunge (A4-F#4-C#4-A3) landing the 2-5 pair G#3-C#4 on
the floor.

**b20-32, arpeggio subversion by stages** (the passage is a graded catalogue):
1. b20-21: root-position launches, but extended THROUGH THE NINTH: C#4-E#4-G#4-B4-**D5**
   (the b9 again) - and the ninth resolves at the TOP: D5 -> C#5 across the 21/22
   barline. Root arpeggio, altered summit.
2. b22-24: the answers dissolve downward - A5-F#5 falling pair, the stepwise third
   A4-G#4-F#4-E#4 (E# = accidental-laden downstroke begins), the D6 five-note scalar
   plunge, then b24's mixed descent C#6-A5-F#5 (arpeggio) melting into E5-D5-C#5 (scale):
   an arpeggio that turns into steps mid-stream.
3. b25: the fully subverted ascent - starts OFF THE ROOT on B# (vii-dim7 of V: B#-D#-F#-A),
   rises through the dim7, **steps DOWN a semitone mid-ascent** (A5 -> G#5), then vaults a
   5th to D#6.
4. b26: the mirror - a descent with an interior UP-step (G#5 -> A5) and **C-double-sharp**
   (the neighbor law again, now enforcing D#'s semitone neighbor at depth).
5. b27-28: arpeggio fully dissolved into the chromatic-tinged downstroke
   D#5-B4-A#4-G#4-F#4-E#4, cadence - and b8's diatonic tail returns CHROMATICIZED:
   E4-D#4-D4, four semitones sliding into b29's C#4.
6. b29-32: reconstitution as the zigzag - up 6th, down 3rd, up 5th, down 3rd, up 6th
   (C#4-A4-F#4-C#5-A4-F#5): the triad traversed in alternating strokes, tonic bars
   alternating with V7b9-fragment bars (b30: E#-G#-B-D again) at one-bar grain.

**The recurring outside note is a character:** D-natural-over-C# (the b9) appears at b5,
b21, b30 - the piece's signature dissonance, always resolving down by semitone. The
double accidentals (D##, C##) are both neighbor-law enforcements. Nothing chromatic is
decorative.

**b33-36, the varied return at pitch level:** same turn cell, but the heads walk DOWN the
triad (F#5-D5-B4-F#4, mirroring b1-4's rise F#-B-D), the harmony becomes a one-bar-per-
station falling-fifths chain (the A7 bar contains the piece's only G-natural), and A#4/E#4
appear as the chromatic tail-notes of the new stations - region-entry by planted
accidental.

## Bach: fixed-point counterpoint and the region-entry accidental

**Head motif (pickup + b2):** rise 3-4 into 5 (C#5-D5 | E5), drop a FIFTH to the tonic
(A4), leading-tone turn (G#4-A4), then the OCTAVE VAULT A4->A5. Descent to the tonic,
tonic confirmed by semitone, then the register claim. The counter-statement (b4) shows
the piece's minimal variation unit: the MIDDLE is identical (A4-G#4-A4 turn), only head
and tail are re-pitched (E5->D5 head, A5->E5 tail) - body constant, endpoints redirected
to the half-close on 5.

**b6-7, the fixed-apex slide cells:** three slide cells (two slurred rising steps + a
detached drop) all reach the SAME APEX A5 while their launches FALL BY THIRDS: F#5, D#5,
B4. So the cell's span widens minor 3rd -> diminished 5th -> minor 7th - the same
widening-interval intensification as Wiedemann's accent leaps. The D#5 is the piece's
first chromatic, and it is doing the Bach version of the neighbor law: **planting the
next region's tension inside a familiar figure before the region arrives** (the strain
cadences on E at b9). Same device at b11: a single E#5 dropped into an A-major arpeggio
run (C#6-A5-E#5, the falling dim 4th) announces F# minor two bars before its cadence;
D#/E# then saturate b12-16 and vanish at the b17 cadence. Every tonal region in the
piece is entered by its leading tone appearing as a melodic surprise first.

**The cadence-octave bar:** b9 (E5-B4-E4), b17 (F#5-C#5-F#4), b45 (E5-B4-E4 in the minor)
- one interval skeleton (octave split as 4th+5th, falling) at three tonal stations, each
carrying the next phrase's pickup in its final beat.

**b22-27, one sigh cell in four couplings** (why nothing here is a block):
1. b22: BRAIDED - accented slurred falling 3rds (B5-G#5, G#5-E5) alternate with tongued
   rising 2nds (E5-F#5, B4-C#5): a net descent assembled from ascents, sequencing down.
2. b23-24: CHAINED - slur-pairs of falling 3rds (D5-B4, B4-G#4, C#5-A4, E5-C#5...) with
   tongued interjections, entries stepping down through E7/A space.
3. b25: LIQUIDATED - the tongued element removed: eight pure slurred falling 3rds
   (E-C# | D-B | C#-A | E-C#), articulation subtraction as intensification.
4. b26-27: COMPOUNDED - a FIXED DYAD C#5-D5 on top while the entries walk down the E7
   arpeggio beneath it (B4, G#4, E4 - falling thirds again), bottoming at D4-C#4 and
   climbing the dominant back up (E4-G#4-B4) into the cadence. The fixed-apex trick of
   b6-7 inverted into a fixed-dyad-over-falling-bass.

**Bourree II's pitch character vs I:** II's head ARCHES DOWN from the third
(A5-B5 | C6-B5-A5-G#5-A5) where I rose to the fifth and vaulted - and II's interval diet
is seconds (scalar waves: b33-35's six-step rise B4->F5 and four-step fall; the b31/49
full-octave downstroke that rhymes the two strains) where I's was thirds, fourths, and
octaves. The modes differ in interval CONTENT, not just color. II-B saturates the
turn/mordent cell (x-y-x) through its regions - G major (b38-40), the D#-turn at the
B-major half cadence (b41), the C-natural/C# cross-relations (b42-43) - the one cell
carried through four tonal stations. The homecoming inverts the sigh: b50's rising
step-pairs (C-B, D-C, E-D, F-E as ASCENDING appoggiaturas), then b51 rebuilds the scale
from E4 with raised 6/7 (F#4-G#4 - the same melodic-minor screw-up as Wiedemann b12)
into the final cadence.

## Kietzer: the slur-pair as a free-interval gesture, planes as harmony

**The cell's interval is a variable.** The slurred pair takes six different interval
forms in the first three bars alone: falling step (F#5-E5, appoggiatura-resolution),
rising OCTAVE (E5-E6, the same gesture as register transfer), falling 6th (E6-G#5),
rising step (G#5-A5, resolution inverted), a six-note arpeggio dilation (b4's
B5-A5-F#5-D5-B4-F#4), and the rising TWELFTH (D4-A5, the overblow). One gesture, free
interval - the pedagogy and the composition are the same fact.

**The repetition planes spell the harmony one tone at a time:** E (5) -> A (1) ->
D#/F# (V-of-V zone) -> E ... Each hammered plane is a chord tone; the slur cells are the
connective tissue that moves between planes; and the PLANE-CHANGE intervals are
patterned and widening across the form: octave (b2), twelfth (b4), two-and-a-half
octaves (b19's E6 -> C#4).

**Chromatic planing:** b6 moves the plane ITSELF by semitone - G#-plane to A#-PLANE (the
A# pointing at B) - discharging into b7's chromatic downstroke B5-A#5-A5-G#5-F#5 (the
same accidental-laden-downstroke species as Wiedemann b26-28). b24 hammers C-NATURAL and
D# as planes - the b9 and 3rd of B7: the dominant-of-the-dominant is planted by
repeating its altered tones, not by running its scale.

**The summit is motivic, not just registral.** The appoggiatura pair climbs a ladder
through the form: B5-A5 = 2-1 (b11), D6-C#6 = 4-3 (b18), F#6-E6 = 6-5 (b19). The
altissimo F#6 is the third rung of a laddered sequence rising by thirds - the peak is
the COMPLETION of a motif process, which is why it can be spent at 43% of the form and
answered by the C#4 floor without breaking anything.

**Nested rates:** b12's sequence has pairs falling a STEP internally while their starts
fall by THIRDS (B5-A5 / G#5-F#5 / E5-D5 over E#5 / C#5 / B4 interjections - the E# again:
region-entry accidental for the f#-minor shading). b8 runs falling-3rd pairs (C#6-B5,
A5-G#5, C#6-B5, G#5-F#5) over a fixed E5-then-D#5 inner pedal - fixed floor under
falling pairs, the inverse of Bach's fixed apex. b32-33 hides a chromatic bass INSIDE
the hammering: the half-bar launches walk A4 - C#5 - D5 - D#5 under the fixed A5 plane
(I - I6 - IV - #iv-dim7), resolving to the E run of b34.

**Dominant vs tonic have different interval bodies:** the E7 discharge (b27) is a
two-octave ARPEGGIO down through every chord position (E-D-B-G#-F#-E-D-B-G#-E-D-B); the
tonic answer (b28-29) is a two-octave doubled-note SCALE up. Dominant falls in leaps,
tonic rebuilds in steps - the piece's largest gestures are harmonically typed.

## The recurring pitch devices, named concretely (with all citations)

Not one law - a device kit these composers share:

1. **Semitone-integrity enforcement**: a motif's neighbor/appoggiatura interval is held
   invariant by accidentals at each new station (Wied A# b3, D## b5, C## b26; Bach's
   grace-turn G#-A vs II's G#-A retained across modes; Kietzer's chromatic snake
   neighbors b25).
2. **Region-entry by planted leading tone**: the next key's tension note appears inside a
   familiar figure BEFORE the region (Bach D#5 b6 -> E; E#5 b11 -> f#; Kietzer E# b12,
   C-natural/D# planes b24 -> B7; Wied A#/E# tails b33-36).
3. **Widening-interval intensification**: the same cell's characteristic leap grows on
   each return (Wied accent-leaps 3rd->4th->5th b9-11; Bach slide-cell spans
   m3->dim5->m7 b6-7; Kietzer plane-transfers 8ve->12th->2.5 octaves; Kietzer's
   appoggiatura ladder 2-1 -> 4-3 -> 6-5).
4. **Fixed-point-plus-moving-point inside one line**: fixed apex over falling launches
   (Bach b6-7), fixed dyad over falling bass entries (Bach b26-27), fixed plane over a
   chromatic bass walk (Kietzer b32-33), fixed floor under falling pairs (Kietzer b8),
   stepwise feet under arpeggiating peaks (Wied b9-11).
5. **Arpeggio subversion menu** (Wiedemann b20-32 is the reference catalogue): extension
   through an altered ninth resolving at the top; non-root launch; interior contrary
   step inside the sweep; arpeggio dissolving into scale mid-stream; chromaticized
   downstroke; zigzag reconstitution.
6. **Cadence tails as motif seeds**: Wied b8's tail (A-G#-F#) inverts into b9's rising
   foot and returns chromaticized at b28 (E-D#-D); Bach's cadence-octave bar recurs at
   three tonal stations; Kietzer's cadence-bar shape (8 sixteenths + quarter + rest)
   closes three different phrases at three registers.
7. **A signature dissonance as a character**: Wiedemann's D-natural-over-C# (b9 of V) at
   b5, b21, b30, always resolving down by semitone; Kietzer's hammered altered tones;
   Bach's dim-4th drops onto new leading tones (C#6-A5-E#5).

These are the actual order-makers. The rhythm-level regularity is the CANVAS; the pitch
devices above are what the listener follows.
