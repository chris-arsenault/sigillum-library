# The Harmonic Mislead — survey (a single line that disguises the progression beneath it)

Research capture for the melody library card `MEL3_MISLEAD`. The technique: a single melodic
line whose strong beats DO NOT plainly hand you the chord root, so the ear hears one harmony (or
one key/tonic) and is later corrected — the line "resolves where the ear doesn't expect," or
withholds the root entirely until a reveal. This is the deliberate inversion of the library's
default (`melody_theory_foundations.md` §3: "the downbeat carries the root… a strong beat on the
3rd/5th/7th is an *intentional* mislead/color, used knowingly"). The mislead is that footnote
made the whole point of a card.

Sources fetched this run are cited inline; the richness (named mechanisms, the WHY, repertoire,
exact pitches) is preserved deliberately — this is a durable library reference, not a checklist.

---

## 0. THE FAMILY — four distinguishable mechanisms (do not conflate them)

The searches surfaced FOUR genuinely distinct ways a single line can lie about its harmony.
They are NOT the same trick; a strong mislead card can stack several, but they must be told apart.

1. **The chord-tone mislead (5th/3rd/7th on the downbeat).** The most local form. The strong
   beat lands a chord tone that is *not the root* — the 5th, 3rd, or 7th — so the bare skeleton
   reads as a different, more stable chord than the one actually sounding. Ear hears the root it
   expects (the note as root of *its own* triad); the accompaniment reveals the real chord. The
   existing draft of this card lived here (5th-lilt + hung-7th). Local, bar-by-bar.

2. **Common-tone reharmonization (the pivot note).** A SINGLE held or repeated pitch is
   reinterpreted as the chord shifts beneath it: same note, new harmonic role each chord. This is
   the most striking single-line mislead because the *line literally does not move* while the
   meaning under it slides — the ear keeps re-hearing the one note. [Icon Collective; Piano With
   Jonny] Worked example (note **E**, held/repeated):
   - over **C** → E is the **3rd**
   - over **Am** → E is the **5th**
   - over **E**(maj) → E is the **root**
   - over **A♭+** → E is the **♯5**
   - over **Dm7** → E is the **9th**
   - over **F**(maj) → E is the **maj7** (F–A–C–E)
   The mislead: hold E, and the listener cannot tell you the key until the chord arrives — and the
   composer keeps re-answering with a *different* chord than the last one implied. (Wikipedia
   *Common tone (chord)* confirms the definition: a pitch class shared by two or more chords; the
   reharmonization application is the songwriting extension of it.)

3. **The emergent / fragile / absent tonic (the key-level mislead).** [Open Music Theory,
   *Fragile, Absent, and Emergent Tonics*] The line opens on chords that *sound like tonic* but
   aren't, and the true tonic is withheld:
   - **Fragile tonic** — the tonic IS present but systematically weakened: it appears only in
     *first inversion* or as an incomplete passing chord, never in root position at a structural
     downbeat, so it never gains authority. (Sufjan Stevens, "Oh God Where Are You Now": the G♭
     tonic only ever shows up in first inversion / as a passing chord.)
   - **Emergent tonic** — the song begins on *other* chords, especially the **relative
     major/minor**, which masquerade as home; the real tonic is revealed late (usually the
     chorus). ("Let It Go," Frozen: F aeolian verse → E♭ mixolydian prechorus → **A♭ major
     chorus** is the true tonic; the verse sounded self-sufficiently minor.) The relative-key
     ambiguity is the engine: C major and A minor share all seven pitches; "it comes down to
     emphasis" — which note the melody anchors, the metric placement, the chord context decide
     the tonic. [Ethan Hein; OMT] So a line can spell what sounds like a complete vi-centered
     phrase and then be revealed, by one chord, to have been in the relative major all along.
   - **Absent tonic** — the tonic is *implied by convention but never sounded*; perpetual
     ambiguity, the home chord is a hole the ear keeps falling toward.

4. **The irregular / deceptive resolution (the cadence-level mislead).** [Wikipedia *Irregular
   resolution*] A dominant-functioning chord resolves somewhere other than its tonic, so the
   melodic leading-tone "resolution" lands on the wrong root:
   - **Deceptive cadence V–vi (major) / V–VI (minor)** — "the most important irregular
     resolution." The ^7→^1 melodic pull is honored *melodically* (the line steps up to what
     feels like ^1) but the root underneath is vi/VI, so the arrival is a chord-tone of the wrong
     chord (the expected tonic note becomes the 3rd of vi). The ache is the gap between melodic
     closure and harmonic non-closure.
   - **Type I** — V7 root descends a minor 3rd (C7 → C♯dim-ish, two common tones, two voices
     by half-step). **Type II** — root rises a minor 3rd. **Type III** — root moves a tritone
     (**tritone substitution**: C7 resolving as if to G♭). All keep two common tones; the melody's
     resolution note is preserved while the bass lies about where home is.

## 1. THE WHY — what the mislead BUYS musically

- **Coolness / restraint / sophistication.** Withholding the root is the harmonic equivalent of
  understatement; it is the sound of jazz and noir precisely because it refuses the obvious
  landing. The ear leans in because it has not been told where home is.
- **A line that "travels" without moving.** Common-tone reharmonization lets a near-static melody
  feel harmonically eventful — the motion is *under* a still line (the Schenker/"let the harmony
  travel" idea, foundations §3, taken to its limit: the line barely moves and the chords do all
  the work, but you only HEAR the line).
- **Surprise that is structural, not random.** The deceptive/emergent reveal is the theory-exact
  version of "earworm + a surprise" (DOCTRINE): establish an expectation (this is the tonic / this
  resolves to I), then substitute. The surprise is an *absence* — the expected root is the one
  thing that does not arrive.
- **Tension held open.** Absent/fragile tonics keep a phrase unresolved, suspended — useful where
  the music must not "sit down."

## 2. WHERE IT LIVES (repertoire across idioms)

- **Jazz/standards:** the home idiom — ii–V's that sidestep the I (deceptive turnarounds), tritone
  subs, melodies pitched on 9ths/13ths/maj7ths so the head never states a bare root. Reharmonizing
  a standard so a single melody note is re-cast as 3rd→♯5→9 of successive chords is bread-and-butter
  (Piano With Jonny; "play any note with any chord").
- **Film/noir & cool jazz:** the withheld-root sound is the genre signature (smoky, suspended).
- **Pop:** emergent-tonic choruses ("Let It Go"); verse-in-the-relative-minor that the chorus
  re-reads as major (and vice versa) — "surprisingly common." [OMT]
- **Common-practice/Romantic:** deceptive cadences (Bach chorale evaded PACs, the Romantic V–vi
  sigh), common-tone diminished and common-tone augmented-sixth chords (a held melody note over a
  chromatic chord that shares it), enharmonic pivot modulations.

## 3. NOTE-LEVEL DESIGN NOTES (for composing the card)

- **Put the root in the bass/chords, NOT on the melodic strong beat.** The whole effect requires
  the accompaniment to state the real root while the line withholds it — the renderer's soft
  block-chord track IS the reveal. The strong-beat skeleton must spell the progression *as the
  non-root chord tones of each chord*. If a downbeat lands a root, the mislead leaks.
- **The pivot note must be metrically strong and long enough to be re-heard.** A common-tone
  mislead only reads if the note is held / re-struck across the chord change (agogic weight,
  foundations §7) so the ear has time to re-interpret it. A flicker doesn't register as a pivot.
- **Resolve the deception in silence.** DOCTRINE: rests are accents; a mislead that lands and then
  goes quiet lets the wrongness *ring* and the ear catch up. Coolness is space, not density.
- **Honor the melodic tendency while betraying the harmonic one.** In the deceptive/emergent
  reveal, the line should still move *as if* resolving (step up to the apparent ^1, sigh down ^2–^1)
  — the lie works because the melodic gesture is honest while the bass is not.
- **Keep it singable and shaped.** Still one apex, still a deep line — but the deep line can target
  the *fragile/emergent* tonic, so the Urlinie descent arrives on a pitch the ear has not yet been
  told is home. The reveal can be the deep line landing exactly when the true tonic chord finally
  sounds.

## 4. THE CARD'S CHOSEN ANGLE

The existing draft worked mechanism **#1** (chord-tone mislead, 5th-lilt + hung-7th) with a #4
deceptive ending. To compose at full craft and genuinely distinct, the new card centers the two
mechanisms the draft did NOT exploit: **#2 common-tone reharmonization** (a single pivot pitch
re-cast under shifting chords) driving toward **#3 an emergent/relative-minor tonic** — the line
opens sounding settled in the relative minor, a held pivot note slides through three harmonic roles,
and the *true major tonic* only emerges at the cadence, the root withheld from every melodic strong
beat until the very last reveal. Mechanism #4 (deceptive turn) seeds the mid-point so the reveal is
earned. This keeps the family's identity (root-withholding, sly, spacious) while teaching a
different lie than the existing draft.
