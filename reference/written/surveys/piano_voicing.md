# Piano Voicing & Comping Survey (library-build source + craft reference)

The cure for the defect heard in Movement IV's C version: a chord realized as a **low, close,
4-note tertian clump in block half-notes**, with a bass note carrying a close 4th. Produced by a
research sweep (jazz-piano pedagogy + psychoacoustics). This is THEORY REFERENCE only — voicings are
HAND-COMPOSED, never generated by a function (a generator stamps the same shape = bland repetition).
Specimens (each demonstrates one mechanic): **PV1 open voicing/voice-leading · PV2 inversions
(descending-bass progression) · PV3 the voicing-type catalog on one chord · PV4 locked-hands /
Barry-Harris · PV5 drop-2 in motion (descending cycle + ii-V-I) · PV6 comp rhythm** — hand-written
DSL card specimens under `technique_library/dsl/cards/voicing/`. Checklist used by EAR.
Pitches in scientific notation, middle C = **C4** (MIDI 60).

One acoustic fact underlies all of it: **the harmonic series is spaced wide at the bottom, tight
at the top** (ascending partials: P8, P5, P4, M3, m3…). Every rule mirrors that. The failure mode
(a low close 4th, block chords) violates it directly.

## 1. The Low-Interval Limit (LIL)
Each interval has a lowest pitch below which it turns to mud, because of **critical-bandwidth
roughness** (Plomp & Levelt 1965): in the bass a 3rd/4th spans fewer Hz than the cochlea's
critical band, so its partials beat. Consonance is **register-dependent**. Charts disagree by ~an
octave; use the conservative jazz-arranging set (Pease/Pullig):
- **2nds (m2/M2):** keep the bottom note **above ~C4**.
- **3rds (m3/M3):** above **~C3** (E♭3).
- **P4:** above **~A2**.
- **P5:** down to **~C♯2**.  **Octaves/unisons: no limit.**
- **Acceptable low:** octaves, open 5ths (go deepest), and **open 10ths** (the way to get a 3rd's
  color down low without mud). The user's instinct — "a low bass note shouldn't carry a 4th,
  octaves at best" — is exactly right.

## 2. Rootless voicings (Bill Evans / Red Garland LH)
Omit the root (the bass has it); stack **A-form = 3-5-7-9**, **B-form = 7-9-3-5**; alternate A↔B so
inner voices barely move. Why: the root inside a close low voicing muddies the 3rd/7th; dropping it
frees a voice for color (9/11/13) and keeps the voicing clean. **Register:** bottom note D3–A3, span
C3–A4 (Levine: "LH pinky between C3 and C4"). Pick the form that keeps the m9's 9-against-♭3 m2
above middle C (the B-form's m2 muds just below it).

## 3. Shell voicings (guide tones) — root + 3 + 7
The 3rd + 7th alone define chord quality; root anchors function. Minimal voicing for sparse textures
(ballad intros, behind a soloist, bebop LH). Guide-tone lines move by step (7→3) into the next chord.

## 4. Spread / open voicings — drop-2, open 10ths
**Drop-2** (drop the 2nd voice from the top an octave) opens a close 4-note chord to a 9th/10th span
— fuller, clearer, modern. **Open 10ths** in the LH (root + 3rd-an-octave-up) get 3rd-quality down
low without mud. Spacing law: adjacent **upper** voices ≤ 1 octave; the **bass-to-chord gap may be
large** (a 10th–2 octaves) — big gaps belong low where the series is naturally wide. Guard drop-2 by
ear: if it sinks a voice into the mud band, don't — keep the closer voicing or lift it an octave.

## 5. Quartal / "So What" & upper-structure triads
**Quartal** (stacked P4ths; Evans's So-What = E-A-D-G-B) gives a rootless, open, modal-ambiguous
color — planes beautifully (4ths are tonally non-committal). **Upper-structure triad** = a plain
triad in the RH over the dominant's 3-7 tritone shell in the LH (e.g. D/C7 = C13♯11) — rich altered
color, controlled because the tritone fixes function and the upper triad is itself consonant.

## 6. Comp rhythm (NOT block half-notes)
The defining feature of comping is **rhythm, not voicing**. Block whole/half-notes on downbeats =
static wallpaper that telegraphs the meter and kills momentum. Devices: **Charleston** (beat 1 +
"and of 2"), reverse Charleston, **anticipations/pushes** (strike an 8th ahead of the beat), **space**
(silence is structural — comp in bursts), rhythmic independence from the melody, and **fills** that
answer in the soloist's gaps. Per bar pick from {charleston, rev, anticipate3, push, sparse, synco,
hold, rest}; don't repeat a cell > ~2 bars; place attacks off the melody's downbeats.

## 7. Planing (Debussy) done right
Move a fixed voicing shape in parallel so it glides as one color. Keep it **open and mid-register
(~C4)**, not a sinking low cluster: when planing downward, widen the spacing or transpose up rather
than letting the clump muddy. (This is the fix the Movement IV romance "planed 9ths" needed.)

## VOICING CHECKLIST (use to critique a piano part — also the agent-loop voicing pass, orchestration_procedure)
1. **Mud:** any close interval (m2/M2/m3/M3/P4) below its LIL? (low 4th = fail.)
2. **Open the low 3rd** → 10th, or move the 3rd up an octave.
3. **Spacing shrinks bottom→top** (wide low, tight high)? Inverted = fail.
4. **Adjacent upper voices ≤ 1 octave** (bass-to-chord gap may be large).
5. **Root redundancy** (doubling the bass's root low+close)? → rootless + a color tone.
6. **Guide tones present** (3rd + 7th define the quality)?
7. **Voice leading** by nearest tone / common tones / 7→3 by step (not parallel root-position blocks).
8. **Rhythm** comped, not block half/whole notes on downbeats.
9. **Register fit** in the C3–C5 sweet spot (not the <C3 mud band, not thin >C5).
10. **Planing** stays open + mid-register.

## REGISTER MAP (middle C = C4)
| Region | Pitches | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Deep bass | A0–C2 | octaves & open 5ths only |
| Low | C2–C3 | P5, P4, **open 10ths**; shell roots; guide tones reach up |
| **Sweet spot** | **C3–C5** | rootless LH (bottom D3–A3); quartal/So-What (~C4); drop-2 (~C4); planed shapes; RH upper-structure triads C4–C5 |
| High | C5–C8 | melody, sparkle, top of upper structures; close intervals safe here |
