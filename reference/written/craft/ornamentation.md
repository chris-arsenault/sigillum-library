# ANNEX ornamentation — Ornamentation & Melodic Embellishment (verbatim research)

## 1. The Taxonomy, as Deterministic Notation Recipes

Each recipe is an editing pass over an existing melody note N (the "main note").

**Accented (long) appoggiatura** — *the expressive lean.* Insert the upper (or lower) diatonic
neighbor ON the beat, stealing half the value of N (two-thirds if N is dotted); slur the pair;
the appoggiatura takes the dynamic stress, N is the release. A half-note G becomes quarter A ->
quarter G, slurred, A on the beat. Romantic practice writes this out in full-size notes — do
this in the pipeline (real durations, slur, optional accent/tenuto on the dissonance). Use on:
phrase peaks, downbeats of "sigh" gestures, any 4-3, 9-8, 6-5 resolution over the harmony.
The single highest-yield ornament for Mahler-style expressivity.

**Unaccented appoggiatura / grace appoggiatura** — small unslashed grace note before the beat;
melodically smooth (steps into N). The lean as flavor, not rhythmic event.

**Acciaccatura (crushed grace)** — slashed small eighth, fast BEFORE the beat, slurred to N.
Prepend a slashed grace one diatonic step below (most common), or above, or a chord-tone leap;
N keeps full value and stress. Use for snap/energy onsets, repeated-note refreshment,
folk/JRPG flavor.

**Mordent (upper/lower)** — execution N-neighbor-N, fast, on the beat. Written-out recipe
(safer for playback fidelity): replace N's onset with two 32nds (N, neighbor) + remainder,
slurred. Lower mordent on a long note is the signature "bite"; in maqam-flavored writing put a
lower mordent using the LOWERED 2nd (Phrygian-dominant on D: D-Eb-D) at long-note onsets.

**Turn / gruppetto** — immediate (upper-N-lower-N) or DELAYED between notes: N held, then the
four-note turn leading into the next note. Written-out recipe for a turn between two notes a
step apart: shave the last beat-fraction off N, insert upper-N-lower as a triplet or four 32nds
gliding into the next note. The delayed inter-note turn is the elegant one — Mozart slow
movements, Tchaikovsky ballet solos, Debussy. Chromatic-lower-neighbor turns sound instantly
"Romantic."

**Trill** — alternation with the diatonic upper neighbor; Classical/Romantic start on the main
note. End with a Nachschlag termination: two small graces (lower neighbor-main) at the trill's
end, or written out as two 32nds resolving to the next note. CADENTIAL TRILL: melody note over
V (typically scale degree 2 trilled with 3), lasting the dominant's duration, termination 1-2
to tonic. TRILL CHAINS at climaxes: trill each note of an ascending line (half-bar each) or
stack trills in 3rds across two flutes/violins — the standard shimmer-to-arrival at structural
dominants (late Beethoven made trills structural).

**Slide / Schleifer** — two or three small graces ascending stepwise into N, before the beat,
slurred. For portamento proper (strings): slur + "port." between two written notes; reserve for
solo violin/cello entering a long high note — once or twice per movement maximum.

**Nachschlag** — ends a note rather than begins one: one or two small notes after N, taking
time from N's tail, slurring into the next note. Recipe: shave a 16th off the end of a long N
and step up-or-down to it. The classic trill-exit and a beautiful standalone "release" figure.

**Written-out vs symbol — pipeline rule:** WRITE OUT appoggiaturas, turns, mordents,
slides-as-runs, Nachschlaege (real durations + slurs); use symbols/small notes ONLY for
acciaccaturas, grace-note groups, and trills with the tr line. Deterministic MIDI + clean
Dorico import.

## 2. Composer Fingerprints to Steal

- **Tchaikovsky (ballet):** solo winds carry near-vocal coloratura — delayed turns at phrase
  ends, acciaccaturas refreshing repeated notes, chromatic scale-flourish "curtsies" linking
  phrases; ornament density rises in cadenza-like solo bars, drops to zero when tutti enters.
- **Rimsky-Korsakov (Scheherazade):** the solo-violin theme is a written-out arabesque — long
  anchor note, then florid descent in free runs over a harp chord; each movement reopens with
  the ornamented cadenza. Recipe: long anchor -> unmeasured-feel run (tuplet 32nds) circling
  neighbors -> land on chord tone.
- **Debussy (Faun):** gruppetti dissolved into the line — the flute's chromatic winding IS the
  ornament; tied grace figures (graces tied across the beat), whole-tone turns, ornaments at
  QUIET dynamics. Steal: turn figures written as quintuplets/septuplets so they float off grid.
- **Mahler:** appoggiatura-saturated melody — nearly every downbeat of the Adagietto is a
  leaning dissonance resolving by step; plus portamento marks for strings. Pass: on every
  strong beat where melody = chord tone, consider replacing onset with upper neighbor.
- **John Williams:** grace-note "snaps" on brass fanfares — slashed grace (often a 4th or step
  below) crushed into accented trumpet notes; 32nd adornments inside triadic fanfare motives;
  horn rips (written gliss into a high note) as macro-ornaments.
- **Uematsu / JRPG:** To Zanarkand's single unslashed graces stepping into melody tones —
  sparse, one per phrase, lower-neighbor, pre-beat, piano dynamic. Sparseness IS the
  fingerprint: one grace note makes the phrase human.
- **Maqam-to-orchestra:** lower mordent on long notes using the mode's characteristic step
  (b2 in Hijaz/Phrygian-dominant), slides into sustained notes, trill-with-flat upper neighbor,
  melisma bursts (4-6 note runs) between phrase notes. Assign to oboe/English horn/solo violin.

## 3. Placement Checklist (run per phrase)

1. PHRASE PEAK: accented appoggiatura (from above) on the highest note — or trill if held.
2. LONG-NOTE ONSETS (>=2 beats): mordent, acciaccatura, or slide. LONG-NOTE EXITS: Nachschlag
   or turn into the next note.
3. CADENTIAL APPROACH: trill on 2 over V with termination; at major structural cadences and
   climaxes, trill chain or stacked trills.
4. REPEATED NOTES: acciaccatura or mordent on the 2nd/3rd repetition (never the first).
5. SECOND STATEMENT of a theme: ornamented reprise — embellish the return, not the exposition
   (Mozart K.457 Adagio written-out variants; Chopin nocturne reprises). On the reprise apply
   recipes 1-4 plus fill one leap with a run.
6. NEVER ornament the first statement of a leitmotif; the ornament is information about
   RETURN and INTENSIFICATION.

## 4. Instrument Idiom Table

- Flute, clarinet, solo violin: everything; fast gruppetti, trills, runs — arabesque carriers.
- Oboe: expressive appoggiaturas and single mordents yes; long fast trills and dense
  passagework sparing (breath/embouchure).
- Brass: grace-note snaps, rips, single short trills (lip trills only on horn/trumpet upper
  partials — avoid low trills, muddy on tuba/low trombones); no turns at speed.
- Harp: NO real trills (use bisbigliando — enharmonic whisper-tremolo); grace-note arpeggio
  sweeps before downbeats are its native ornament.
- Timpani/perc: flams (single grace) and drags (double grace) before accents.
- Tutti strings: section trills fine; section graces fine; portamento only solo or deliberate.

## 5. Density Rules

- Solo line: up to 1 ornament per bar at climaxes, 1 per 2-4 bars normally. Tutti: 1 per
  phrase (4-8 bars), preferably unison grace-snaps or section trills only.
- Never two different ornament types on adjacent notes; minimum 1 beat plain after any ornament.
- Density tracks the dramatic curve: exposition sparse -> development moderate ->
  reprise/climax dense -> coda sparse.
- Cap ornamented onsets at ~10-15% of melody notes in a solo line, ~3% in tutti.
