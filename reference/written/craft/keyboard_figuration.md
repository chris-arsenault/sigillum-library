# ANNEX keyboard_figuration — Keyboard Writing & Figuration Craft (the missing reference layer)

Why this exists: the texture crisis (reference/written/craft/texture_diagnosis.md). "Rachmaninoff bell
voicing" entered the pipeline as prose and exited as root-fifth-third quarters.
This annex records the NOTE-LEVEL standard. It feeds the canonical technique library
(`technique_library/INDEX.md`); cards cite sections here. For orchestral rhythm/
dialogue/doubling/voicing research see orchestral_rhythm/chord_scoring/dialogue_doubling (not duplicated).

## 1. The nocturne texture (Chopin model — the grief/love default)

THREE TO FOUR REAL VOICES, not two:
- MELODY: vocal, top; at returns harmonized in 3rds/6ths/octaves (the "Chopin melody
  is more of a texture than a single line").
- BASS: slow, "almost sluggish" — but a LINE (lowest note of each LH sweep forms a
  walking/descending contour, not static roots).
- WINDING MIDDLE VOICE: "adds a great inner agitation" — the LH's upper notes and/or
  RH thumb form a real moving inner part marking the pulse, weaving around the melody.
- (4th voice optional: tenor countermelody, often LH thumb.)
LH undertakes "more than merely rolling chords — counter-melodies, harmonic pedal
points, delicate inner voices." The middle voice is what separates a concert nocturne
from a student one.

## 2. The Rachmaninoff elegy LH (the bell-grief model)

- LH arpeggio span: TWO-PLUS OCTAVES per bar (Elegie op.3/1: contra-Eb up to Bb'),
  CONTINUOUS motion (8ths or triplets — 6-12 attacks/bar in the LH ALONE), evenness
  across the span is the point.
- The figure's crest follows the phrase: it rises as the melody rises; the top note
  of the sweep is itself a slow inner melody.
- Bell chords (chord_scoring s4): strong fundamental + emphasized minor-3rd partial HIGH in the
  chord, attack fused, allowed to DECAY — bells are an EVENT (climaxes, tollings),
  not a default accompaniment grid.
- Climax texture: both hands chordal, melody in octaves with inner doubling, LH
  leaping bass octaves + afterbeat chords.

## 3. The Piano-Collections transformation doctrine (the genre standard)

- The stated aim: "consider the groundwork of individual compositions in order to
  TRANSFORM these pieces into piano arrangements, rather than simply performing music
  from the game on the piano."
- RH harmonizes the melody "in thirds, sixths, octaves"; LH "develops arpeggios and
  more elaborate formulas."
- The published failure mode (= our diagnosis, verbatim): "failure to acknowledge
  timbre and texture as essential components of piano composition, resulting in dull
  arrangements focused on clear melody and simple harmonic accompaniment rather than
  exploring the rainbows of light and texture possible across the keyboard."

## 4. Figuration variation law (keyboard and orchestra alike)

- The FIGURE is a theme too: it varies at every phrase boundary (crest shape, span,
  rhythm subdivision, register) while keeping its identity — exactly R10's logic
  applied to accompaniment.
- Advanced LH figuration: chromatic passing inside the sweep; spans larger than an
  octave; open-position breakups (3rd displaced up an octave); broken octaves
  (murky) for storm; the same chord NEVER voiced identically in consecutive phrases.
- INTENSITY DIAL inside one figure family: halves -> quarters -> 8ths -> triplets ->
  16ths (orchestral_rhythm s1A) — a 20-bar section traverses the dial, it does not sit on one value.

## 5. Brahms inner-voice law (the orchestral mirror of s1)

- "Inner voices MOVE while outer voices hold long notes"; inner-voice motives
  animated in 8ths, written as REAL counterpoint (separately stemmed thinking).
- Accompaniment is built from invertible material: triple counterpoint — the bass
  usable as melody, its inversion as bass, the theme as accompaniment.
- Operational: any part labeled "pad" must contain a moving tone (suspension chain,
  neighbor figure, or exchanged voice) at least once per 2 bars — else it is FLOOR
  and must be declared as such.

## 6. The union-surface law (orchestral_rhythm s2, restated as a hard rule)

Union of all layers' attacks ≈ a continuous surface rhythm (8ths or 16ths per the
section's pulse class) in every non-void window — WHILE no single layer plays it all.
For solo keyboard the law applies to the two hands together: melody long note =>
the other hand (or middle voice) moves. This is the single cheapest test that
separates composed accompaniment from token accompaniment.

## 6b. THE FIGURATION TAXONOMY — the universe beyond arpeggios
(added after the user caught batch 1 defaulting 7/8 motion layers to chord-spelling)

A figuration stratum picks its TYPE first; broken-chord is ONE of eight:
1. BROKEN-CHORD (arpeggio/Alberti/sweep): spells the harmony. The default — and
   therefore RATIONED: <= 1/3 of a movement's figuration layers.
2. REPEATED-NOTE / PULSE: repeated chords or single notes (Schubert hammering,
   Beethoven-7 dactyl cell, toccata notes, measured tremolo). Rhythm-identity, not
   pitch-contour; the dynamic shape carries the phrase.
3. COMPOUND MELODY: ONE line implying 2-3 voices by registral zigzag WITH
   voice-leading (Bach solo-string doctrine; Chopin middle voices). Each implied
   voice moves by step when it returns. The true anti-arpeggio: looks like a line,
   functions as counterpoint.
4. SCALAR CURRENT: stepwise runs connecting chord tones; tirades; measured scales,
   at cadences in parallel 3rds/6ths.
5. NEIGHBOR OSCILLATION: rocking 2nds, trill-as-texture, murky broken octaves,
   pedal oscillation — motion that circles, never spells.
6. DOUBLE-NOTE: parallel 3rds/6ths moving STEPWISE (Brahms double-note texture);
   octave passages.
7. CHORDAL-RHYTHMIC: full chords where the RHYTHM is the figure (dactyl-spondee,
   off-beat stabs, strum patterns), re-voiced per bar (chord_scoring s6).
8. DISPLACED OSTINATO CELL: a melodic (non-chordal) cell with accent/length
   displacement per repetition (Stravinsky method, orchestral_rhythm s4).
LAW: a movement's figuration plan names the TYPE per layer per section; adjacent
sections never reuse the same type in the same role; type 1 rationed as above.

## 6c. THE PIANO TECHNIQUE UNIVERSE (added: "you're missing an entire universe")

Gesture/attack techniques (each is a card or a future card):
- STRUM / ROLLED CHORDS as texture: written-out rolls (streams staggered by 32nds so
  every note RINGS), strum-rhythm patterns with direction changes — guitar/harp
  rhetoric on the piano.
- SYNCOPATED COMPING: off-beat chords, ties across the barline, anticipations, over
  an on-beat bass groove.
- TRIPLET CURRENTS: triplet textures incl. 2-in-one-hand vs 3-in-the-other WITHIN the
  single player.
- DOTTED RHYTHM FAMILIES: overture sharp-dotting (double-dots at the return), the
  long-short LILT (siciliana class), SCOTCH SNAP (reverse dot) at phrase ends.
- PROCLAMATION BREAK: lyric line ABRUPTLY interrupted by fortissimo rhetoric (unison
  octaves / hammered blocks), silence, then the lyric resumes as if unshaken — the
  recitative-interpolation gesture.
- CROSS-HAND SWEEP: one gesture passed LH->RH across 4+ octaves; LH crossing OVER
  (m.s. sopra) to strike bells above a working RH.
- ALTERNATING CASCADE: hands alternate 16th groups down/up the keyboard (waterfall).
- TENOR MELODY: the song in the LH baritone register, RH halo above, bass kept by
  LH leaps (art-song / stride lineage).
- THUMB MELODY: melody in the MIDDLE of one texture (RH thumb), figuration above,
  bass below — three decks, one player.
- ORCHESTRAL TREMOLO: measured both-hand tremolo as orchestra-on-the-piano under
  octave melody.
- MARTELLATO OCTAVES: alternating-hand octave hammering, migrating accents.
- CARILLON/CAMPANELLA: one repeated bell pitch above a moving texture.
- HAZE PLANING: parallel 7th/9th chords drifting una corda, melody floating above
  the blur.
- JAZZ COMP / WANDERING HAND: the comping hand lives BETWEEN states — single root,
  partial dyad (3/5 or shell 3/7), short connecting run (3-4-5), full stack (1/3/5)
  — syncopated (charleston placements, anticipations), guide tones voice-led across
  changes (7th falls to the next 3rd); melody WANDERS above: off-grid starts,
  chromatic approach tones, register drift, unhurried.
- WALKING COMP (piano-trio-in-one): LH true walking quarters with chromatic
  approaches; RH syncopated comp chords (rootless/shells); sparse melody on top.
Not yet carded (future, need Dorico-side notation): glissando, sostenuto-pedal
layers, silently-depressed-key resonance, clusters.

## 6d. THE RHYTHMIC SENTENCE LAW (added: "your compositions lack movement and
grace... nothing that matches triplets with dotted into a 16th run... it's all
'here's 4 bars of triplets'")

Rhythm is a LANGUAGE; a phrase is a SENTENCE with varied word-lengths:
- A melodic/figuration phrase traverses >= 2-3 VALUE CLASSES inside itself (e.g.
  triplet group -> dotted figure -> 16th run -> arrival long note). Uniform-value
  bars are a deliberate special effect (motoric engine identities), never the
  default speech.
- Rests are words too: gestures break MID-BAR (the 8th-rest inside the comp), lines
  enter off the grid, pickups of 16ths launch the next chord.
- The sentence passes BETWEEN hands/voices: the dotted reply in the bass answers
  the triplet question in the treble; arrival in one voice = motion in another.
- THREE-PART COUNTERPLAY: with 3 voices, the conversation includes interruption
  (B speaks in A's rest), borrowing (B takes A's tail note and bends it), role
  inversion (the bass takes the question in augmentation while the treble comps),
  and hocket (one composite line split across all three) — rhythmic AND harmonic
  counterplay, not stacked strata.
- DEPLOYMENT LAW (cards vs pieces): the library's one-technique-per-card format is
  EXPOSITION ONLY. In actual movements, textures MIX at phrase level: no single
  card-texture occupies more than ~4 consecutive bars unchanged; every 8-bar span
  shows >= 2 value-class regions and >= 1 mid-bar gesture break. G17 gains a
  sentence check (value-class count per phrase) when cards lock.

## 6e. THE HIGH-WATER MARK (judgment rule, NOT a hard floor — user-locked)

P17/P18/P19 define the COMPLEXITY BAR for the whole work: rhythmic sentences,
cross-voice counterplay, gestures that break and resume. This is a JUDGMENT
standard, applied per section when composing and at review:
1. Does this movement contain passages that REACH the mark? (Every movement should
   have stretches a listener would call intricate, conversational, alive.)
2. Does the movement's center of gravity SKEW toward sentence-class writing?
   (Mixed-value phrases as the normal speech, not the exception.)
3. Is every SIMPLE passage simple ON PURPOSE? Silence, proclamation, a bare line
   declaring a theme, a held breath — all legitimate and necessary — but the
   narrative must NAME the simplicity as a choice ("bare because the theme is
   being declared"), or it is presumed to be the old default and recomposed.
4. The question at review is never "did it pass a floor" but "would a musician
   praise the CRAFT of this passage" — the machine indicators (value classes,
   gesture breaks, 0%-repetition stretches) only POINT at where to look.
5. THE BAR IS NOT A RHYTHM (user correction: "you've taken complexity to mean
   triplet run followed by dotted then 16th and are using that pattern
   consistently... there are millions of permutations"). The triplet->dotted->16th
   sentence is ONE shape. Complexity has many AXES, and a passage can earn the bar
   on any of them:
   - RHYTHMIC: mixed values, syncopation, displacement, elision, asymmetric phrase
     lengths (3+5, 2+3+3);
   - CONTRAPUNTAL: voice independence, imitation, invertible counterpoint;
   - HARMONIC: suspension chains, reharmonization of a repeated figure,
     voice-leading richness under a static surface;
   - REGISTRAL: travel, crossing, spacing events;
   - TEXTURAL: role changes mid-phrase, density curves, color handoffs;
   - CONVERSATIONAL: interruption, borrowing, role inversion, hocket.
6. DIALECT CONSISTENCY DOCTRINE: rhythmic consistency is GOOD — a complex rhythm
   needs repetition to be learnable — so each movement picks a SMALL set of
   signature sentence-shapes and complexity dialects AND DERIVES THEM FROM ITS OWN
   THEMES (the movement's melody cells generate its sentence rhythms: M5's cells
   -> V's sentences; M4's riff cells -> III's). The V sentence-shape belongs to V.
   Movements do not inherit another movement's signature shapes; at each new
   movement's Stage 0 the dialect set is declared fresh.

## 6g. DIALOGUE FORMS — AN OPEN FIELD, NOT A LIST
(rev 2 after the user: "i wasn't trying to give you laws to implement, i was trying
to get you to expand your creativity... what you instead did is went hyper specific
on the example i gave and made it so there is no room for anything else.")

This section is INCOMPLETE BY DESIGN and must stay that way. User examples are
sparks; the response to a spark is: SEARCH the space, harvest parallels across
traditions, then INVENT forms fitted to the movement's own dramaturgy. The palette
below is a starting harvest, never a boundary:
- Equal trading at fixed lengths (the jazz trading-fours family); CUTTING-CONTEST
  variant: each response tries to TOP the call.
- Halving dialogue to a unison convergence (sawal-jawab family — carded as X1).
- Broken call / pre-emption / role flip (carded as X2).
- Circulating chain with colored repeats (carded as X3).
- LEADER/CONGREGATION: the response is FIXED (a refrain), the call transforms
  freely around it — and the fixed element can carry the drama (its color darkens,
  or one day it fails to come).
- CORO-PREGON INVERSION: the CHORUS is the repeating element and the lead
  improvises in its gaps (montuno logic — the inverse of gap-fill).
- ESCALATION-BY-HEAT (qawwali family): the response repeats while the call
  intensifies each cycle — building by fervor, not by compression.
- VERTICAL DIALOGUE (organum family): no alternation at all — one voice holds the
  slow fixed line while another comments floridly ON it in real time.
- INTERLOCK-AS-FUSION (kotekan family): two voices fill each other's rests at
  speed until they form ONE composite line — dialogue dissolving into unity.
- SMALL-VS-MASS (concertino/ripieno): dialogue of scales and weights, not phrases.
- RESPONSORIAL vs ANTIPHONAL: soloist-to-choir vs choir-to-choir are different
  dramas; collective grooves can ANSWER a lead (second-line logic).
- AND THE MOVEMENT'S OWN: every movement derives dialogue forms from its
  dramaturgy (IV: teller/tale/listeners — narration vs ENACTMENT; the tale getting
  ahead of its teller; listeners who stop answering).
The one principle kept from rev 1: an answer that parrots is not an answer.
Everything else here is vocabulary to be exceeded.

## 7. The card doctrine

If a texture is not worth composing at note level once (as a card), it is not worth
shipping 16 times (as a section). Cards are composed, machine-measured (vocab,
voices, span, repRhy), auditioned by the user, LOCKED, then threaded — the melody
pipeline's law extended to every layer of the music.
