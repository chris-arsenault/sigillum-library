# ANNEX melody_primacy — Melody Primacy: what makes themes hummable (verbatim research)

## 1. The earworm findings (Jakubowski et al., APA/Goldsmiths, 3000-participant INMI dataset)
- Earworm tunes have MORE COMMON global melodic contours than non-earworms — the generic
  shapes of pop/folk: most common = "Twinkle Twinkle" arch (first phrase RISES, second FALLS).
- BUT they pair the generic shape with UNUSUAL local events: distinctive leaps or repeated-
  note figures ("Smoke on the Water" opening, "My Sharona" riff, "Bad Romance" chorus).
  FORMULA: common global contour + one uncommon local feature.
- Faster average tempi than non-earworms. Rhythm regularity supports motor/subvocal rehearsal.

## 2. Mario overworld (Kondo)
- "The notes never stray far from their root chord — THE RHYTHM DOES ALL THE HEAVY LIFTING":
  swing, syncopation, off-beats, duple melody against percussive triplets (grooves in 2s and
  3s at once).
- Opening = a 4-note instantly-recognizable cell built on REPEATED NOTES: E-E-E / two fast
  notes, a gap, the same note again. The hook is rhythmic identity on near-static pitch.
- Singability: simple, repetitive, easy to hum — complexity lives in chord-melody tension,
  not in the surface.

## 3. Aerith's Theme (Uematsu)
- A STRONG 4-BAR MELODY stated first, then repeated in variation throughout — melody is the
  structure, not decoration over a progression.
- Yearning = gently falling-and-rising contour; bittersweet = I–v under it.
- Drama = saved leaps: P4 up to the submediant (b25), then P5 up to the tonic (b29) at the
  emotional peak. Leaps are RATIONED — spent only at the climax.
- Solo flute carries it; oboe joins in duet counterpoint — one voice owns the tune.

## 4. One-Winged Angel (Uematsu)
- Built MOTIF-FIRST: "three or four musical phrases each day," then assembled "like a
  puzzle." Identity = short cells (diminished tetrachord ostinato, transposed up a semitone
  into Phrygian; chant = simple REPEATED singing) — not melody-over-progression.
- Stravinsky Augurs model: ostinato + displaced accent as the theme itself.

## 5. Zelda / Tetris
- Zelda: heroic DESCENDING pattern in parallel minor; repetition with segments REORDERED
  and phrase lengths varied to dodge squareness while staying recognizable.
- Tetris (Korobeiniki): short TWO-SECTION theme, simple, infectious, recallable years later.

## 6. Uematsu doctrine
- "Music that could be remembered after just ONE listen." Central melody first; recurring
  motifs carry character arcs. The NES 5-channel limit forced melody to carry everything —
  the catchiness came FROM the constraint.

## 7. Craft synthesis (songwriting/scoring pedagogy)
- 3-4 notes anchor a piece. The hook is "a meme for your melody": short, repeatable,
  emotionally loaded.
- Antecedent-consequent (question-answer) 4+4 phrasing.
- Stepwise motion = the singable path; leaps = rationed surprise at peaks.
- Repetition establishes the hook; variation retains attention. Arc across the phrase.

## OPERATIONAL RECIPES (the melody law — binding on every theme)
R1 THE CELL: every theme reduces to a 1-bar cell of 2-4 pitches with a rhythmic identity
   (repeated note, anacrusis snap, syncope, or dotted figure). The cell IS the meme.
R2 SHAPE: phrase contour = common arch (rise then fall, or Zelda-descent) + exactly ONE
   uncommon event: a leap >= P4 at the peak, or one foreign/flat tone. Not more.
R3 QUESTION-ANSWER: themes are 4+4 (or 2+2): consequent restates the cell and resolves
   the cadence the antecedent left open.
R4 RHYTHM FIRST (clapping test): the theme must be identifiable from its onset pattern
   alone. If clapped rhythm could be any theme, there is no theme.
R5 REPETITION BUDGET: the cell sounds >=3 times within the 8-bar statement, verbatim or
   sequenced — immediate restatement, not "development."
R6 SINGABILITY: range <= a 10th; mostly stepwise; core statement uses values a person can
   whistle at tempo (>= 8ths; ornaments excepted); no harmonic dependency for identity.
R7 BARE TEST: the theme works completely unaccompanied. Sing it into a phone test.
R8 FOREGROUND LAW: while the theme speaks, accompaniment recedes (dynamics one tier down,
   register cleared, no competing motion in the melody's octave). Mario mix: tune on top.
R9 ARPEGGIO PROHIBITION: a theme may OUTLINE a chord only if rhythm carries the identity
   (Mario E-E-E). Arpeggiated figuration is accompaniment, never theme.

## 8. MELODY ARCHITECTURE (R10) — the clause model, from Reign of The Simmered solo vn 95-118
A MOTIF is a word. A THEME (8 bars) is a sentence. A MELODY is a PARAGRAPH: 16-32 bars,
start-middle-end, saying something. Studied structure (verbatim from the score):
- 16-bar build = FOUR 4-BAR CLAUSES: A (ramp-head launches -> held arrival -> ONE striking
  leap (B->F) -> stepwise descent -> breath in halves) | A' (SAME head compressed, different
  middle, cadences LOWER -- the counter-question) | A-8va (clause 1 verbatim AN OCTAVE UP --
  escalation by register alone) | B (departs the pattern, circles to the SUMMIT, holds it a
  full bar -- the period).
- 16-bar proclamation answers LOW (2 octaves under the summit -- contrast), rising wave
  shapes each cresting higher, then the LONGEST note of the phrase + leading-tone lift ->
  arrival an octave above its own start.
RULES:
R10a Every clause = subject (the cell/head) + continuation + breath (longer values at the
     clause end). No clause is rhythmically uniform bar-to-bar.
R10b Clauses are RELATED: shared head-DNA, varied middles; escalation clauses may be exact
     octave transpositions; cadence pitches PLAN the drama (down, down, up, SUMMIT).
R10c The paragraph ends on a SUSTAINED arrival prepared by the longest note and/or a
     leading lift. The summit appears ONCE.
R10e R6's range cap applies PER CLAUSE; the paragraph may span wider through escalation.
R10d Movements state the full melody at least once uninterrupted (the foreground law R8
     holds for all 16-32 bars). Motif/cell reuse elsewhere = quotations FROM the melody.
