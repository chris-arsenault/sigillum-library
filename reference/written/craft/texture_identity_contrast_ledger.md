# Texture Identity & Contrast Ledger — Phase-A Contract for Directed Fractalization

This annex gives Phase A a shared way to guide both continuity and contrast across
multi-agent movement writing. Its central rule:

**Fractalization means added musical specificity on the assigned dimension;
density is one possible detail mode.**

Each section receives a positive musical job. The next agent elaborates that job
with greater specificity: exact line shape, exact silence, exact reserve, exact
color handoff, exact rhythmic sentence, exact register path, exact tutti grammar,
exact decay, or exact seam behavior. Detail can be crowded or exposed; the ledger
names which dimension becomes more specific.

## Research Grounding

- Texture has vertical and horizontal dimensions: how many voices/instruments sound
  and how active their rhythms are. A strong plan tracks density and rhythmic
  activity separately.
- Effective melody/accompaniment writing depends on timbre, register, and rhythmic
  activity relationships; foreground and background clarify each other through
  assigned difference.
- Orchestration articulates form through the rate and degree of timbral change.
  A section boundary gains force when color, register, mass, or motion changes at
  the scale of the form.
- Auditory grouping research treats orchestration as control over concurrent,
  sequential, and segmental grouping. The ledger uses those perceptual axes:
  which sounds fuse at once, which continue as streams, and which events mark
  phrase/form boundaries.
- Register relief, high-only or low-only windows, hollow textures, chamber windows,
  soli/tutti shifts, and true rests are positive orchestral events.
- Homorhythm is an exclamation mark. Its power comes from placement, preparation by
  rhythmic convergence, and release into a new texture state.
- Cards are vocabulary; movements speak sentences. The ledger chooses the sentence
  job and detail target first, then agents search cards that fit.

## Ledger Axes

Each mid-level section row declares these axes. Values are short prose. The lists
below are prompts for invention.

1. **Continuity identity.** The through-line this section carries from the movement:
   theme cell, harmonic gravity, swelling pad, gait, timbral signature, bass law,
   dialogue character, register image, reserve color, seam rhetoric.
2. **Section job.** What this section contributes to the form: declaration,
   continuation, transition, answer, closing, development, crux, clearing, return,
   coda, interruption, afterimage, seal, preparation, release.
3. **Fractalization target.** The dimension downstream agents make more specific:
   line, rhythm, color, register, silence, density, counterpoint, tutti, decay,
   seam, reserve, harmonic rhythm, figuration, dialogue.
4. **Detail mode.** How the target grows in detail: mixed-value sentence, hocket,
   antiphonal relay, long-line contour, exact tacet/reserve curve, color morph,
   register bloom, chamber exposure, tutti convergence, pedal release, composed
   gap, family accretion, family reduction, attack halo, resonance wake.
5. **Motion source.** Who creates the felt movement: lead rhythm, counterline,
   bass, offbeat chord layer, 16th current, triplet current, percussion cell,
   color handoff, harmonic rhythm, register travel, rests/cuts.
6. **Rhythmic identity.** Dominant value classes and sentence behavior: long-line
   mixed values, clipped rests, dotted cells, syncopated/offbeat, 2-vs-3,
   displaced cell, 16th run, triplet current, homorhythmic hit, held breath.
7. **Bass behavior.** Walking line, pedal/drone, tacet/window, afterbeat/oom-pah,
   ostinato cell, registral plunge/lift, suspension bass, unison tutti, sparse
   punctuation, bass-free high texture.
8. **String role.** Lead, sustained pad, active inner, tremolo bed, pizzicato
   punctuation, divisi chorale, soli/ripieno contrast, tacet/window, 16th current,
   double-note counterline, harmonic halo.
9. **Wind/brass role.** Lead, counterline, chorale, fanfare/proclamation, antiphonal
   choir, color halo, offbeat support, stinger/punctuation, withheld/reserved,
   event-only doubling, tutti convergence.
10. **Keys/percussion/harp role.** Structural attack, color halo, current, comp
    rhythm, bell event, snare/cell subject, destination/convergence crash,
    tacet/reserve, resonance wake.
11. **Density and space.** Active-part range, chamber window, full mass, exposed
    solo, family absence, resonance tail, register gap, silence as event.
12. **Register and color.** High-only, low-only, middle chamber, full pyramid,
    hollow gap, wide-open luminous, compressed/close, register sweep, extreme
    reserved for a named destination or convergence event.
13. **Reserve role.** A force/color being saved as a positive dramatic identity:
    trumpet crown, cymbal/tam-tam detonation, tuba floor, chorus/body, echo timbre,
    solo desk, full strings, low brass, harp cascade, coda blaze.
14. **Contrast relation.** What this section newly contributes relative to its
    neighbors, stated as a positive role: "motion moves to wind relay," "bass
    becomes pedal gravity," "strings become register bloom," "keys become attack
    halo," "brass becomes public edge," "texture becomes chamber exposure."
15. **Library search terms.** Category/facet terms and candidate card families for
    the section's positive job.
16. **Required audible event.** One event the section makes concrete: cut, tutti
    sentence, hocket window, bass drop-out, color handoff, pedal release, antiphonal
    compression, high-register-only answer, coda-crown entry, resonance wake.

## Movement-Level Review

These are orchestrator and merge review questions. They enter composing briefs
after translation into positive assignments.

- Which axis is the movement's identity, and where does it carry through?
- Which axis changes at each mid-level boundary?
- Which sections elaborate density, and which sections elaborate exposure,
  reserve, silence, color, register, counterpoint, or seam behavior?
- Which recurring card family or texture family functions as identity?
- Which recurrence receives a new detail mode?
- Where does high-water-mark complexity live: rhythm, counterpoint, harmony,
  register, texture, conversation, or a combination?
- Where are the movement's mass event and exposed/window event, when the form uses
  full-orchestra breadth?
- What is the movement's destination state, where does it occur, and how is it prepared, reached,
  transformed, released, withheld, or answered?
- Which axes stay separate — dynamic maximum, density maximum, registral maximum, harmonic arrival,
  tutti arrival, percussion spend, melodic high point, and formal destination — and which deliberately
  converge?
- How does this movement's destination placement and texture profile differ from the other current
  movement mid-level briefs?

## How Phase A Uses It

- **A1** names the movement's continuity identities and broad contrast needs.
- **A2** authors the ledger row per mid-level section at role/behavior level.
- **A3** expands each row into per-4-bar verbs and figuration plans, naming the
  active fractalization target and detail mode for each group.
- **A4** sketches frame lines that support the ledger's continuity identities and
  leaves local detail mode available for Phase C where the section agent owns it.
- **Section agents** compose freely inside their row, elaborating the assigned
  fractalization target.
- **Merge/review** checks the realization against the ledger at section level:
  continuity identity, section job, detail mode, reserve role, contrast relation,
  and required audible event.
