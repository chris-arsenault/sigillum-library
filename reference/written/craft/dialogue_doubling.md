# ANNEX dialogue_doubling — Call-and-Response & Strategic Doubling (verbatim research)

## A. DIALOGUE TECHNIQUES

### 1. Antiphony (choir vs choir)
Lineage: Gabrieli's cori spezzati — spatially separated choirs trading call and response, the
first stereo music — straight into modern practice. Functional units in a modern orchestra:
the three "choirs" (strings, woodwinds, brass; percussion/harp/keys as a fourth color-choir).
Surviving rules:
- Each choir HARMONICALLY SELF-SUFFICIENT during its statement (complete ensembles, not
  fragments).
- OVERLAP THE SEAMS: responder enters on the caller's final note (dovetail), sustaining
  momentum.
- ESCALATION: trade long phrases (4 bars), then shorten the exchange interval (2 bars, 1 bar,
  half-bar stretto) toward TUTTI CONVERGENCE. Compression-to-union is the standard antiphonal
  climax engine.
Film/game: Williams' Throne Room (Walton-esque) — trombone/horn fanfare ANSWERED by full-
orchestra statements (low brass call, high brass + strings response). Uematsu's Victory
Fanfare compresses the same gesture: unison trumpet flourish (call) -> harmonized full-brass
cadential answer. CONVENTION: fanfare call = unison/octaves, rhythmically incisive; response =
harmonized, sustained, wider-voiced. Call carries rhythm + pitch identity; answer carries
harmony + weight.

### 2. Phrase-gap fills
The Classical fill (Mozart): winds get brief interludes at phrase ends when the principal
voice rests or sustains; by the late 1770s strings and winds operate as two independent
ensembles in dialogue. Kondo/JRPG loops use the identical mechanism — melody's built-in gaps
answered by a countermotive (which also disguises the loop seam). Big band (brass fills behind
a sax soli) translates directly.

WHERE fills go (mechanical):
- Under any melody note >= 2 beats (the long-note rule).
- In the gap between phrase end and next phrase start.
- NEVER during fast melodic motion — fills compete only with stasis.
- Fill must end (or decay) before the melody re-activates: figure only while melody is ground.

WHO answers (contrast rules):
- Different FAMILY from the caller (strings call -> winds answer; brass call -> strings).
- Different REGISTER — answer above a mid/low melody, below a high one; avoid caller's octave.
- LIGHTER weight than the caller (solo or pair answers a section) unless building to climax.
- ONE consistent answerer identity per section — the dialogue reads as two characters,
  not a crowd.

### 3. Echo techniques (four orthogonal axes — combine freely)
- DYNAMIC echo: f phrase restated p (Baroque terraced). Cheapest, always works; same
  instruments acceptable.
- REGISTER echo: 8va up (ethereal, receding into light) or down (shadowed, gravity/menace).
- TIMBRAL echo: same notes, same register, NEW instrument (oboe echoed by muted trumpet).
  The most "magical"; muted brass and harmonics are the classic echo-timbres.
- DIMINUTION/TRUNCATION echo: echo only the tail, or at half values. Reads as memory/decay;
  ideal for transitions. (Temporal-displacement doublings at very short delays simulate
  acoustic echo — Lutoslawski.)

### 4. Dialogue forms
- TRADING 2s/4s: strict alternation of 2- or 4-bar units between two fixed sections; keep the
  harmony continuous underneath so trades feel like one argument.
- MELODY MIGRATION (Mahler relay / Klangfarbenmelodie): one continuous line handed instrument
  to instrument MID-PHRASE. Rules (per Mahler 6 analyses): at most two timbres carry the
  melody at any instant; incoming instrument OVERLAPS 1-2 NOTES with the outgoing (cross-fade,
  not splice); hand off at breath points or long notes; each color change motivated (register
  entering the new instrument's sweet spot). Programmatically: split where the line crosses
  register boundaries; overlap one note at the joint.
- HETEROPHONIC DIALOGUE: two instruments "discuss" the same line with different ornaments
  simultaneously — dialogue and doubling at once (see section 8).

## B. STRATEGIC DOUBLING

### 5. Mixed-unison recipe table

| Recipe | Character / use |
|---|---|
| Fl + Ob unison | Round, sweet, full; oboe edge softened — generic lyric wind melody |
| Fl 8va above Vln | Silvery sheen on string melody; classic "A-theme glow" |
| Cl + Vla unison | DARK VELVET; melancholy interior melodies, mid-register |
| Ob + Vln unison | Reedy edge; pastoral, slightly nasal intensity |
| Hn + Vc unison | NOBLE, warm, heroic breadth (Rimsky: "beautifully blended, soft") |
| Bsn + Vc unison | Rich tenor cantabile; standard Romantic cello-line reinforcement |
| Cl/Fl + Vln unison | "Takes the bite off the strings" — smooths, mellows |
| Tpt + Ob unison | Brilliant, hard, ceremonial edge |
| Vla + Hn / Vln + Tpt | Rimsky's sanctioned brass-string unisons; martial gleam |
| Picc + Glock (8va/15ma) | Crystalline glitter crowning a tutti |
| Cel + Hp + Fl staccato | SPARKLE; magic/music-box topic |
| Hp + pizz strings | Plucked halo; clarifies attacks |

Rimsky's doctrine: PURE colors for solos and exposed lines; unison mixtures create new
composite "virtual instruments" — but fusion requires each partner to compromise toward the
other (clarinet limits volume to match oboe; oboe softens attack). He disliked same-instrument
octave pairs (2 flutes in octaves): register inequality unbalances them.

### 6. Octave-doubling strategy (dynamic-tiered)
- p-mp: melody in 1 octave, ideally one color.
- mf-f: 2 octaves (vln I + vln II/fl 8va).
- ff tutti: 3 octaves, families interlocked.
- HIGHLIGHTING: add the 8va doubling ONLY at the phrase peak — upper octave enters for the
  climactic 2-4 notes, then drops out. Cheaper and more vivid than doubling throughout.
- 15ma CROWNING: piccolo (+/- glockenspiel) at the extreme top only at ff climaxes (Rimsky
  reserves piccolo's top register for the sustained climax of Scheherazade). Two-octave gaps
  with an empty octave between read as two separate instruments — fill the middle octave in
  tuttis.

### 7. Shadow / halo doublings (partial doublings)
- RHYTHMIC SHADOW: sustained instrument (horns, low strings) doubles only the downbeats/
  structural tones of a moving line — orchestrated reverb (Stravinsky's horn prolonging
  selected piano notes, Symphony of Psalms).
- ATTACK HALO: harp (or pizz) doubles only the attack-points of a legato string melody —
  definition without weight.
- SPARKLE HALO: glockenspiel/celesta doubles a wind line's main beats 8va-15ma above.
- PUNCTUAL DOUBLING: winds accent selected string notes (Messiaen) — emphasis, not mass.

### 8. Heterophony
The same line simultaneously plain + ornamented — maqam ensembles, gamelan ("10 decorative
variations of one theme at once"), gagaku. Debussy imported it after the 1889 gamelan
("Pagodes"); Janacek's speech-melody layers work similarly. RECIPE: sustained plain version in
strings/horn; florid version (turns, graces, scalar fills) in solo wind, same register or 8va
up. Keep structural tones synchronized; ornament only between them.

### 9. When NOT to double
- SOLO COLOR PRESERVATION: any unison doubling sacrifices the solo's sharpness, inflection,
  presence — doubled lines drift toward the background.
- CHAMBER CLARITY: fewer simultaneous instruments, added one at a time, creates space;
  over-doubling = "turgid," murky textures.
- GREY-SOUND COST: if everything is doubled everywhere, mixtures stop registering as color
  events. Doubling is a CONTRAST resource — pure color before mixture makes the mixture land.
- Don't double fast inner counterpoint; don't double during another line's solo moment;
  don't doubled-octave a bass line into the melody's register.

## AUDIT CHECKLISTS

DIALOGUE PASS (per movement):
1. Mark every melody note >=2 beats and every phrase-gap >=1 beat. Is >=50% answered by
   something? Is any answered by TOO MUCH?
2. Each fill: answerer a different family AND register from the caller? Exits before the
   melody moves?
3. Any phrase >8 bars in one unbroken color? -> candidate for Mahler relay (overlap 1 note).
4. Any A-section repeating literally? -> convert to echo (pick ONE axis).
5. Climax approach: call-response intervals compress (4->2->1 bars) and converge to tutti?
6. Exactly 2-3 dialogue "characters" per section, consistently cast?

DOUBLING PASS:
1. Classify every doubling: unison-mix / octave / shadow-partial / heterophonic. Justify each
   ("louder" is not a color justification).
2. Dynamic-tier check: octave count matches dynamic (1/2/3 for p/f/ff)? Picc+glock 15ma only
   at ff peaks?
3. Phrase peaks: 8va highlight entering ONLY at the peak? If it runs the whole phrase, cut.
4. Solo lines: zero doubling on designated solo-color moments; the bar BEFORE a solo strips
   mixtures so the pure color contrasts.
5. Grey-sound scan: any 8+ bar span where every element is doubled -> un-double the least
   important layer.
6. Mixture vocabulary: <=3-4 signature recipes per movement, used consistently as IDENTITY
   (e.g., cl+vla for theme B, hn+vc for theme A), not at random.
7. Legato string melodies: consider harp attack-halo. Moving wind lines at climaxes: consider
   downbeat shadow in horns.
8. Octave gaps: no empty-octave sandwiches in tutti melody stacks.
