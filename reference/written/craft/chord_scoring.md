# ANNEX chord_scoring — Chord Scoring: Voicing, Spacing, Doubling (verbatim research)

## 1. Spacing — the overtone-series "pyramid"

Governing law: WIDE intervals at the bottom, NARROWING toward the top, mimicking the harmonic
series of the bass note (8ve, 5th, 4th, M3, m3...). Internal intervals shrinking as you ascend
= resonant; inverted pyramid (close low, wide high) = bottom-heavy and hollow.

LOW INTERVAL LIMITS (lowest clean placement of the interval's BOTTOM note; guidelines —
softer dynamics and darker timbres more forgiving, loud brass less so):

| Interval | Lowest bottom | Interval | Lowest bottom |
|---|---|---|---|
| m2 / M2 | E3 / Eb3 | tritone | Bb2 |
| m3 | C3 | P5 | Bb1 |
| M3 | Bb2 (some say F2) | m6 / M6 | C2 / B1 |
| P4 | Bb2-C3 | octave | contra-C and below fine |

Crucial subtlety (Hoffmann): if the chord's root sounds elsewhere (tuba under a trombone
voicing), check limits AS IF the root were present in the stack. Below ~C2 use only octaves
and (cautiously) fifths. Never a 3rd below C3; never a 2nd below E3.

STANDARD LAYOUTS (root-position major triad):
- 4 voices: bass on root, 5th-8ve gap, close triad above: C2-G3-C4-E4.
- 6 voices: C2-C3-G3-C4-E4-G4 (overtone clone).
- 8 voices (tutti skeleton): C2-C3-G3-C4-E4-G4-C5-E5 — 3rds appear only from C4 up.

## 2. Doubling priority

Double ROOT > 5TH > 3RD. NEVER double: leading tone, the 7th of a seventh chord, any active
altered/chromatic tone (one resolution path -> parallel octaves or smeared resolution). In
mixture chords (bVI, bIII, Neapolitan): double root or 5th, NOT the chromatically inflected
3rd (Neapolitan: double the bass). Dynamics change density, not priority: pp = one instrument
per pitch, doublings pruned; ff = multiply root and 5th in octaves freely, keep the 3rd
represented once or twice in the upper-middle register only (an ff major chord with four
doubled 3rds turns sour). Minor-chord 3rds tolerate one extra doubling. Major 3rds high =
bright; major 3rd in one mid voice = neutral "open" sound.

## 3. Scoring across families — the four methods (Adler, after Rimsky-Korsakov)

1. JUXTAPOSITION/LAYERING: each family owns a register block (WW above, brass below).
   Maximum clarity/color separation; risk of seam. For antiphonal color, terraced textures.
2. INTERLOCKING (crossing): alternate instruments note-by-note (Fl-Ob-Fl-Ob; Tpt1-Hn1-Tpt2-
   Hn2). Best blend; DEFAULT for homophonic tutti chords.
3. ENCLOSURE: one timbre surrounds another (flutes top+bottom, oboes inside). Recessive color
   absorbed; good for taming a reedy middle.
4. OVERLAPPING (dovetailing): families share 1-2 common pitches at the seam (top trombone =
   bottom horn). THE standard fix for the layering seam.

BRASS STACKING: trumpets close on top; trombones close below; HORNS FILL the middle (2 horns
~ 1 trumpet/trombone in weight — double horns or mark +1 dynamic vs heavy brass). Tuba doubles
Tbn3 an octave below or takes the root.
WOODWIND COUPLINGS: score in pairs (2+2), paired 3rds/6ths between like instruments; flutes
weak below C5 — give them octaves/upper doublings; clarinets cover the dead middle; bassoons
double vc/cb or fill tenor 5ths.
STRINGS: multi-stops ff only, prefer open-string chords (D major rings: open D-A-E); sustained
verticals = DIVISI (non-div double stops bulge then decay); spread divisi so each desk's pair
lies within a 5th.

## 4. Color voicings

- add9/add2: keep the 2nd ABOVE C4 and adjacent to the 3rd; below C4 voice the 9th as a 9th.
- QUARTAL: stack P4s from ~C3 upward (C3-F3-Bb3-Eb4-Ab4); double outer voices at 8ve for weight.
- CLUSTERS: climax-only; secundal cluster in ONE family (divisi strings or stopped horns),
  framed by clean octaves in another family above and below (anchors for the ear).
- ORGAN SPACING: complete close-position chord duplicated in 2-3 octaves simultaneously
  (winds = upperwork, low brass = pedal). Even, sustained, no internal hierarchy.
- VANGELIS/PAD TRANSLATION: wide root-5th-10th low + slow-beating close upper triad ->
  orchestra: cb/vc octaves + va/vn2 sustained close triad con sord + flutes doubling 8ve,
  harp p arpeggio supplying the "LFO."
- BELL CHORDS (Rachmaninoff): strong fundamental + emphasized minor-3rd partial high in the
  chord; piano/harp ATTACK fused with sustained strings; let the chord DECAY (not re-attacked).
- LOW END: octaves always safe; bare 5th adds power but locks the chord as rootal — avoid 5ths
  below Bb1.

## 5. Brightness and weight control

DARK: cap the voicing low (top voice below ~G4), top owned by violas/horns/clarinet chalumeau,
omit upper octave doublings, single 3rd buried mid-texture.
BRIGHT: top voice above ~D5 owned by violins/flute/trumpet, top line doubled at the octave,
3rd near the top.
- FFF TUTTI RECIPE (D major): D1(Cb,Tba) - D2(Vc,Tbn3,Cbsn) - A2(Tbn2) - D3(Tbn1,Bsn) -
  A3(Hn2,4) - D4(Hn1,3,Tpt3) - F#4(Tpt2,Vla div) - A4(Tpt1) - D5(Vn2,Ob,Cl) - F#5(Vn1 div) -
  A5 - D6(Vn1,Fl,Picc 8va). Every section internally complete; seams overlapped; timp on D.
- PP LUMINOUS RECIPE: no 16' contrabass; bass at C3 or higher; one instrument per note; flutes
  low / clarinets mid. Mahler/Bruckner pp brass chorale variant: horns+trombones close
  position, B2-G4 band, one per pitch, no trumpets (or cup-muted top). Debussy variant: 6-8
  part muted divisi strings, harp doubling chord tones as harmonics, top = flute or vln
  harmonic.
- LUMINOUS ADD9 TEMPLATE (C): C3-G3-E4(Vla)-D5(Vn2 con sord)-G5(Vn1 harm.)-E6(Fl); harp rolls
  C3-G3-D4-E4-G4. 9th exposed near the top, 3rd kept singular.
- MF WIND CHORALE (Bb): Bsn2 Bb2, Bsn1 F3, Cl2 D4, Ob2 F4, Cl1 Bb4, Ob1 D5, Fl2 F5, Fl1 Bb5 —
  interlocked Ob/Cl, flutes doubling top octave.

## 6. Harmonic rhythm

On every chord change RE-ATTACK AND RE-VOICE the whole vertical in sustaining instruments —
don't hold common tones limply unless you WANT a suspension. Scoring a suspension: give the
suspended note to a DIFFERENT, individually audible color (oboe, horn, solo cello) than the
changing mass; re-attack the mass on the change; sustain only the suspension; resolve it by
step within the same instrument. Fast harmonic rhythm -> simpler, fewer-voice chords; slow
harmonic rhythm earns the full pyramid.

## AUDIT CHECKLIST (run per vertical)
1. Any 3rd below C3? 2nd below E3? 4th/tritone below Bb2? (Include the implied root.)
2. Interval sizes shrink (roughly) bottom -> top?
3. Leading tone / chordal 7th / altered tone doubled anywhere? -> strip.
4. Root in the true bass? 5th-heavy bass below Bb1? -> octave instead.
5. Each family's sub-chord complete and self-balanced (could it stand alone)?
6. Seam between layers: shared pitch (overlap) or interlock present?
7. Horns weighted x2 (or +1 dynamic) vs heavy brass? Flutes below C5 load-bearing? -> reassign.
8. Top-voice owner matches intended brightness; pp = one-per-pitch; ff = root/5th multiplied,
   3rd <= 2x.
9. String verticals: sustained -> divisi; accented -> multi-stop; open strings exploited?
10. On chord change: everything re-attacked/re-voiced; held notes are DELIBERATE suspensions
    in a traceable color?
