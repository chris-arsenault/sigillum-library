# ANNEX orchestral_rhythm — Orchestral Rhythm: Accompaniment Textures (verbatim research)

Grid notation: 8th/16th slots. X = attack, x = weak attack, . = silence, = = sustain,
B = bass note, C = upper chord. 4/4 8th-grid = 8 slots; 3/4 = 6 slots; 16th-grid = 16.

## 1. Catalogue of accompaniment figures

**A. Repeated-chord pulsing (the "engine").** Even reattacks of static harmony. Archetypes:
Schubert (Erlkoenig triplet hammering; Unfinished syncopated pulse), Beethoven 7 Allegretto —
Berlioz: a DACTYL + SPONDEE: long-short-short | long-long (quarter, two 8ths, two quarters),
iterated "relentlessly, either in three parts, or in only one, then in all parts together."
- Even 8ths: X X X X X X X X (anxious, motoric; strings or repeated horn/clarinet chords)
- Even quarters: X = X = X = X = (processional)
- Beethoven-7 cell (2/4, 8th grid): X . x x X . X . — identity strong enough to BE a movement.
- Triplet pulse over duple melody = instant Schubertian urgency.
INTENSITY DIAL: halves -> quarters -> 8ths -> triplets -> 16ths is the classic long-range
crescendo of activity (Rossini crescendo logic).

**B. Oom-pah / after-beat family.** Bass on the beat, chords off it; they NEVER coincide.
- Waltz (3/4): B . C . C . — bass 1, chords 2 & 3 (vc/cb pizz + horns/violas).
- March (2/4, 8th grid): B C B C — bass on 1 & 2, chords on the &s (tuba/low strings + horns:
  the horn after-beat is THE march pad rhythm).
- Polonaise (3/4, 8th grid): X xx X X X X — 8th + two 16ths on beat 1, then even 8ths; the
  accompaniment itself carries the dance ID.
- Polka/galop (2/4): march pattern lighter, pizz bass. Ragtime variant: displace chords a 16th.

**C. Brahms syncopated chords.** Chords ONLY off the beat over on-beat bass: chords
. X . X . X . X (violas/2nd vlns), bass B . . . B . . . Floating motion without busy-ness.
Tied variant ( . X = X = X = . ) blurs the barline entirely.

**D. String figuration types** (orchestral substitutes for keyboard Alberti, which is
idiomatically keyboard and rarely literal in orchestra):
- MURKY-BASS (broken octaves): celli 8ths low-high-low-high; storm/anxiety bass.
- MEASURED TREMOLO: notated repeated 16ths one pitch/dyad — Rimsky's standard sustained-
  excitement device in violins/violas. Unmeasured trem = pure texture, no rhythm.
- OSCILLATING (fingered) TREMOLO / ROCKING 3RDS: 16ths alternating chord tones a 3rd apart —
  the nocturne/barcarolle inner voice ("chosen to elucidate the harmony").
- ARPEGGIATED 16THS: chord swept low->high per beat (1-3-5-8 per four 16ths); flowing water.
- 6/8 ROCKING: B x x B x x (low-mid-high), lullaby/barcarolle bass.

**E. Horn/wind pad rhythms.** Two identities: (1) SUSTAIN pad — held chords, neutral glue
(Belkin: keep held notes in dull/weak registers so they don't compete); (2) REPEATED-OFFBEAT
pad — horns reattack on off-8ths ( . X . X . X . X ), converting the pad into an energy
source. Switching sustain -> offbeat reattack is one of the cheapest excitement upgrades.

**F. Pizzicato walking / punctuating bass.** Pizz quarters every beat = light walking; pizz
on 1 & 3 only = airier. Belkin: "plucked or staccato basses," "aerate the texture with
frequent rests."

**G. Harp placement.** Structural points, not continuous pulse: rolled chord on downbeats of
bars/phrases; arpeggiated sweeps doubling string figuration; single bass octave on beat 1
reinforcing pizz. Avoid dense off-beat harp — decay blurs it.

## 2. The complementary-rhythm rule (operational)

Belkin's perceptual law: "the ear tends to follow change — a moving line surrounded by
instruments holding long notes will stand out." Corollaries: make the solo line MORE
rhythmically active than the accompaniment, or differentiate by timbre/register when activity
is equal. Rimsky's layering (melody / countermelody / harmony / bass / decorative figuration
as separate strata) presupposes the same: each layer audible because each moves differently.

OPERATIONAL RULE: per beat-slot, compute melody activity.
- Melody attacks >= 8ths this bar -> accompaniment sustains or thins (pad, single pizz).
- Melody holds a long note or rests -> accompaniment moves (figuration enters, fill/echo,
  harp sweep, inner-voice motion). The "fill the long note" principle — classic at phrase
  ends, where accompaniment or countermelody answers in the gap (dialogue).
- Avoid simultaneous attack-density peaks in two layers unless an intended tutti hit.
- QUANTITATIVE TARGET: union of all layers' attacks approximates a continuous surface rhythm
  (steady 8ths or 16ths) while no single layer plays it all.

## 3. Homorhythm vs polyrhythm (the tutti unison)

Homorhythm — all parts one rhythm — is the orchestra's exclamation mark: fanfares, cadential
hits, coda hammer-blows, chorales. Its power comes from SCARCITY (Rite of Spring "Augurs"
chords = homorhythm weaponized by displaced accents). Rules: reserve full homorhythm for
<10-15% of a movement — phrase-ending punctuation, the climax, the final cadence; approach by
progressively synchronizing layers over 2-4 bars (rhythmic convergence = built-in crescendo);
exit by re-fragmenting (one layer keeps moving while others drop to sustain).

## 4. Cross-rhythm and metric devices — dosage

- HEMIOLA: in 3/4, two bars regrouped as three 2-beat units. Classic at cadences (Baroque
  convention; Brahms everywhere). Dose: major cadences + 1-2 developmental passages/movement.
- 2-AGAINST-3 / 3-AGAINST-2: Brahms's trademark — melody in triplets over duple accompaniment
  or vice versa (Sym 3 finale: tied triplets over even quarters). Dose: a sustained patch
  (8-16 bars) once or twice per movement, or a persistent secondary-theme identity.
- ACCENT DISPLACEMENT: keep the grid, move the sforzando (Beethoven scherzi). Cheap, high
  impact; scherzo/development.
- STRAVINSKY CELL DISPLACEMENT: fixed ostinato cell with the accent or an added/subtracted
  8th shifting each repetition. It REPLACES complementary rhythm as the organizing principle
  for that section — dose carefully.
- Budget per movement: 70-80% plain-grid stratified rhythm; 15-25% ONE cross-rhythm device;
  homorhythm as punctuation.

## 5. Rhythmic differentiation of strata — rule of thumb

Assign each simultaneous layer a distinct VALUE-CLASS, one per layer, no duplicates: melody =
quarters/8ths (mixed); bass = quarters or dotted-halves; inner harmony = sustained halves OR
off-beat 8ths; figuration = 16ths; punctuation (timp/harp/brass) = sparse downbeats. Two
layers sharing both register AND value-class will fuse or fight — differentiate at least one.
Max ~3 genuinely independent rhythmic strata plus punctuation; beyond that, double rather
than invent.
