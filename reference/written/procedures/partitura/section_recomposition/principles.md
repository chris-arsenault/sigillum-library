# Section Recomposition - Principles And Standing Orders

This procedure recomposes ONE section or passage of an existing DSL piece, surgically.
The full composition standing orders apply
(`reference/written/procedures/partitura/dsl_composition/principles.md` - cardinal
principle, completion standard, source discipline, research routing); read them once
per fresh context. These are the additional laws of surgery:

## Surgical Rule

Keep everything that works; replace only the named defect. The rest of the piece is
frozen: the spine (lead line, bass line, declared harmony/chord track) and every bar
outside the target span stay untouched unless the repair contract explicitly names
them. If fixing the defect seems to require rewriting frozen material, stop and record
that in the pass note as an escalation - do not silently widen the surgery.

## The Defect Is Named Or It Does Not Exist

No recomposition starts from "this section feels weak." The diagnosis names the defect
by bar and part in musical terms - inert accompaniment, unprepared clash, monotone
rhythm, dead seam, unplayable spacing, phantom checkpoint, compile-blocking notation -
with the projection or compile output that shows it. Compile errors from the notation
gates (bar markers, checkpoints, ranges, marks) are legitimate defects: the diagnosis
then distinguishes notation-only repair (re-mark the same sounding music) from musical
repair (the notes themselves change).

## Divergence Rule

Every review cycle, every tier: a divergence from the piece's existing plan, contract,
or material identity is allowed ONLY if documented with why it is *better* - not merely
acceptable - and surfaced in the pass note, never silent.

## Seams Are Part Of The Repair

The bars just before and after the recomposed span belong to the repair's audit even
when they are frozen: pickups, tails, dynamics, and momentum must connect. An abrupt
reset at the splice boundary is a defect unless the contract declares it as the event.

## Feedback Is Structural - Scope At The Cause, Not The Symptom

Review findings and user feedback almost always name a large-scale structural failure,
even when phrased about a moment. Before touching anything, ask "what structural or
process failure is this pointing at?" and restate the defect at that altitude. The unit
of response is the phrase, section, or process - never a 2-4 bar spot patch over a
symptom; one-off point defects are not what a repair procedure is for. A projection or
scanner may locate symptoms but never defines the scope of the response.

This does not contradict the surgical rule - it completes it. Surgical means the
contract's boundary is respected absolutely; structural means that boundary is drawn
around the CAUSE. A contract that patches three bars of a defect whose cause is the
section's accompaniment scheme is the wrong contract, however cleanly it is executed.
Execute structural repairs as a total-coverage editing traversal of the affected
region (every bar read, small deliberate edits, material carried), not as scattered
point fixes and not as a wholesale rewrite - both pendulum swings are failures.

## Improvement, Not Remediation - Countering The Good-Enough Lens

You carry a known bias: you generalize toward not doing work, read music as good
enough, and hunt hard errors instead of improvements. Every review in this procedure
runs in EDITING mode - "what would make this bar better," with keep as the justified
exception - never AUDIT mode ("is it broken"). There is always room for improvement:
a "no change" musical_verdict must name what you tried to improve and why the music is
better without the change. Edit means improve, not delete: deletion feels safe because
new notes can be wrong - that safety is the tell - so every finding names music to
COMPOSE, and a rest opened for breath is paired with music composed in the opened
space. The `improvements` pass-note field records what got compositionally better (or
the best candidate found and why it was or was not taken); it is fed back to you in
later payloads as material to build on.

## The Drafting Lens Decays The Other Dimensions

Whichever dimension led while the original passage - or your replacement - was drafted,
the unattended dimensions decayed while it was written. After recomposing, name what
your drafting lens was, then run deliberate editing traversals of the same bars with
the neglected lenses (total coverage, every bar read). Do not install a fixed ranking
of dimensions to compensate; the crown itself is the bug. Revisiting with an eye for
improvement is the only mechanism that brings the neglected dimensions up to quality -
it is what the seam pass and the closeout audit cycles are for.

## The Ear Is The Verdict

Gates are floors. Sounding projections are primary; declared-intent views only verify
bookkeeping. A repair passes when the recomposed passage reads as music that belongs to
the piece - same engine logic, same identity, better where it was worse - not when the
compiler goes quiet.
