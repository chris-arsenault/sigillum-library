# Section Recomposition - Principles And Standing Orders

This procedure recomposes ONE section or passage of an existing DSL piece, surgically.
The full composition standing orders apply
(`reference/written/procedures/partitura/dsl_composition/principles.md` - cardinal
principle, completion standard, source discipline, research routing); read them once
per fresh context. These are the additional laws of surgery:

## Surgical Rule

Keep everything that works; replace only the named defect. The rest of the piece is
frozen: the spine (lead line, bass line, declared harmony/chord track) and every bar
outside the target span stay untouched unless the repair contract explicitly names
them. If fixing the defect seems to require rewriting frozen material, stop and record
that in the pass note as an escalation - do not silently widen the surgery.

## The Defect Is Named Or It Does Not Exist

No recomposition starts from "this section feels weak." The diagnosis names the defect
by bar and part in musical terms - inert accompaniment, unprepared clash, monotone
rhythm, dead seam, unplayable spacing, phantom checkpoint, compile-blocking notation -
with the projection or compile output that shows it. Compile errors from the notation
gates (bar markers, checkpoints, ranges, marks) are legitimate defects: the diagnosis
then distinguishes notation-only repair (re-mark the same sounding music) from musical
repair (the notes themselves change).

## Divergence Rule

Every review cycle, every tier: a divergence from the piece's existing plan, contract,
or material identity is allowed ONLY if documented with why it is *better* - not merely
acceptable - and surfaced in the pass note, never silent.

## Seams Are Part Of The Repair

The bars just before and after the recomposed span belong to the repair's audit even
when they are frozen: pickups, tails, dynamics, and momentum must connect. An abrupt
reset at the splice boundary is a defect unless the contract declares it as the event.

## The Ear Is The Verdict

Gates are floors. Sounding projections are primary; declared-intent views only verify
bookkeeping. A repair passes when the recomposed passage reads as music that belongs to
the piece - same engine logic, same identity, better where it was worse - not when the
compiler goes quiet.
