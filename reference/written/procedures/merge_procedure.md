# MERGE PROCEDURE merge_procedure v1 — many drafts → one movement (a fresh recompose that carries every draft's best forward)

How to combine several existing drafts of the same movement into one. A companion to
`orchestration_procedure.md` (which composes a movement fresh): this governs the case where two or
more complete drafts already exist and the task is to fuse their strengths into a single coherent
movement. `unified_procedure.md`'s rules/stages/annexes (H/D/J, §1–§3) and the note-list grammar
bind VERBATIM; this governs only the MERGE-SPECIFIC flow. Run it as a multi-agent fan-out, like
orchestration.

## THE CARDINAL PRINCIPLE — carry every draft's high-water mark forward, then raise it

The merged movement holds the best of every source AND adds. For each musical dimension — melody,
harmony, bass, rhythm, orchestration, contrast, seams, reserve — find the draft that realizes it
richest and carry THAT realization forward as the merged movement's FLOOR, re-voiced by hand to the
merged harmony and roster, then elaborated further. The merge is additive and monotonic: each
dimension lands at least as rich as the strongest source, and the whole gains the coherence no
single draft had. Treat every input — the drafts, the frame, each cast list — as a floor to
elaborate far beyond, never a ceiling to write up to. The win condition is a movement that sounds
like one composer who absorbed every draft and surpassed all of them.

## PREMISES THIS PROCEDURE ENCODES

- **The complexity already exists; preserve and compound it.** Each draft contains hard-won detail.
  Name it, carry it, raise it. A merged voice carries at least the rhythmic life, register travel,
  and contrapuntal independence of the best source's version of that voice, plus the merge's own
  additions.
- **Every voice keeps a living, individual musical identity.** The merged texture is a continuous
  union of purposeful parts: accompaniment may be pulse, off-beat reattack, after-beat, staggered
  attack, displaced cell, walking aerated bass, chorale, drone, sustained pad/field, or silence.
  Use the type the music calls for. Pads are valid when they are the intended color/floor/pressure;
  they are not valid as an unshaped default.
- **Motion is a CURVE across the form; dynamism is the goal.** Each section gets a target motion
  level; forward motion is spent where it earns its place and space opens around it so the form
  breathes. The contrast across sections is the sonata's life.
- **The merged form has a journey and destination, not a default climax.** Preserve the strongest
  draft material, but do not let the merge collapse into a last-third swell. Name the destination
  state, place it where the form earns it, and keep dynamic maximum, density maximum, registral maximum,
  harmonic arrival, tutti arrival, percussion spend, melodic high point, and formal destination separate
  unless convergence is the explicit design.
- **A clear melodic foreground projects.** It owns the top octave and the highest attack-density
  over a stratified accompaniment (up to three independent strata plus punctuation); weight is added
  by doubling an existing stratum.
- **Measurement confirms the richness landed in the notes.** Verify the design by measuring the
  realization, not by reading the prose around it (see MEASUREMENT DISCIPLINE).
- **Returns are real note-lists.** The complexity lives in the notes; every voice validates to its
  section's length.
- **Agents refine; the merge fans out.** Analysis, frame, sections, and the orchestration-life hunt
  are each fanned to fresh agents on minimal positive briefs; the centre schedules, threads, and
  measures.

## PHASE M0 — ANALYZE EACH DRAFT (one fresh agent per draft, in parallel)

For each existing draft, an analysis agent reads the whole draft (the actual note-data, not only the
prose comments) and returns a structured profile, citing sections and bar numbers:
- **Form map** — sections, bars, keys, tempo, dramatic function, active-voice count, total length.
- **Harmony** — how the progressions move and voice-lead; the color/chromatic vocabulary.
- **Bass** — where it sings as a line (walking, contoured, contrapuntal); where it grounds as a pedal.
- **Rhythm** — where fast notes and syncopation live; which sections are rhythmically alive.
- **Orchestration** — how the full roster is used; idiomatic writing, doublings, antiphony, register.
- **Internal contrast & flow** — how distinct the sections are; the dramatic arc; the seams.
- **Best sections and weakest sections** — named, with the specific reason each works or falls short.
- **One-paragraph verdict** — the draft's single greatest strength and its single greatest weakness.

The centre reads the craft library in parallel for the evaluative lens (`orchestral_rhythm.md`,
`texture_diagnosis.md`, `texture_identity_contrast_ledger.md`, `texture_craft.md`,
`dialogue_doubling.md`) — what good orchestration and rhythmic stratification look like.

## PHASE M1 — SYNTHESIZE + DECIDE THE MERGE GEOMETRY (with the composer)

- **Scorecard.** Build a per-dimension comparison naming each draft's distinctive strength and its
  characteristic gap. Confirm where the drafts agree (shared form/key-plan/material — what makes a
  merge possible) and where they sit at opposite ends of an axis.
- **Name the axis and choose the point.** State the spectrum the drafts span (e.g. motion:
  relentless ↔ motion-starved; texture: wall ↔ exposed) and choose where the merged movement sits.
  Hold the chosen point as a governing design intent.
- **Choose the geometry with the composer.** Decide together: recompose into one coherent movement,
  preserve a few deliberate contrast seams, or keep identifiable blocks; choose the backbone (a
  source draft's form, or a fresh spine cherry-picking the best section sequence across all drafts).
  Confirm what the composer values in each draft in their own words and hold that as the bar.
- **Choose the destination map.** State the destination state and placement: early revelation,
  midpoint turn, late arrival, final bar, recurring return, plateau, collapse, or withheld arrival.
  If the strongest event lands in the last third, explain why the merged form requires it rather than
  default narrative gravity. Split the axes before section agents write notes.

## PHASE M2 — THE GOVERNING DESIGN LAW (author it, positively, as the merge's contract)

Author the movement's governing texture/motion law as a positive contract that every section agent
implements. It encodes complexity preservation directly:
- **The journey/destination curve.** Assign each section a motion level (sparse, low, moderate, high,
  crest, stripping, broad-then-thin) and a state-change job so the form's dynamism is explicit. Spend
  forward motion where it earns its place, open space where the destination needs lyric clarity,
  exposure, suspension, or aftermath, and reserve fast notes for the sections whose job needs them.
- **Foreground/background balance.** Name the projecting melodic voice per section; stratify the
  accompaniment (≤3 independent strata + punctuation); add weight by doubling.
- **Accompaniment as the right engine.** Compose every accompaniment voice with the identity the
  section needs: interleaving rhythmic figures, sustained field, chorale support, ostinato, drone,
  walking bass, or planned silence. If a sustained pad/field is used, shape its register, color,
  entry/release, harmonic function, and dynamic life.
- **Reserved forces reached by convergence.** Name the reserve (a brass crown, a single cymbal/
  tam-tam stroke, a solo timbre) and the one place it is spent; reach the tutti by synchronizing
  moving strata (convergence = crescendo) and release it by re-fragmenting.
- **Syncopation/cross-rhythm placed where it earns its place.** Install a sustained cross-rhythm
  identity (2-vs-3, off-beat chords, accent displacement) in the transition and development.
- **Cypher rotation.** Stagger entrances and exits so voices enter and clear across several bars and
  across section boundaries, rather than together on the bar line.

Record this as a shared CONTRACT document the section agents read, alongside the frame.

## PHASE M3 — AUTHOR THE FRESH FRAME (one frame agent; a living scaffold)

The frame agent authors, as note-lists and data, the movement's spine drawn from the chosen
geometry: the ROSTER, the SECTIONS (musical seams + per-section length), the per-bar `chord_map()`
realizing the key-drama, the TEMPO plan, the reserve plan, the carry-forward cells (germ, seal,
signature gestures), and — per section — the MOTION entry (level, foreground, strata, fast-note
placement, and the one required audible event). The MOTION table is the contrast-ledger discipline
made load-bearing: each section declares a distinct identity and a concrete event. The frame is a
thin, living scaffold the section agents elaborate far beyond and may reshape to serve the music.

## PHASE M4 — RECONCILE THE MATERIAL (one unified thematic set)

Unify the drafts' thematic material into one set — one germ, one primary theme, one secondary theme
— sourced from the locked materials (`symphony/materials/themes.py`). Where drafts derived a theme
differently, choose the strongest derivation and carry it. Section agents fit material harmony-first
and stay free to reshape it to serve the music (material is a living input).

## PHASE M5 — COMPOSE THE SECTIONS (one fresh agent per section; carry the best forward)

Each section agent receives: its MOTION job, the frame contract for its bars, the governing design
law (M2), the thematic inputs, the boundary state of its neighbors, and — the merge-specific
ingredient — the NAME of the source draft whose realization of this section's material is strongest,
to study and carry forward. Each agent:
1. **Carries the high-water mark forward.** Study the cited source's realization, take what is
   strong, and re-voice it by hand to this section's harmony and roster — then elaborate it several-
   fold (the cited gesture is a floor).
2. **Composes to the motion job.** Realize the section's level, foreground, and event; give every
   sounding voice a living musical identity; build the full texture the job asks for (sparse,
   lyric, drive, crest, stripping, sustained field) so it elaborates far beyond the frame's skeleton.
3. **Lets the foreground project** over the appropriate accompaniment: stratified figures, chorale,
   ostinato, pad/field, drone, or planned silence; places fast notes or sustained weight where the
   job assigns them.
4. **Composes the seams as living joins** — momentum carried through, pickups handed off, caesuras
   cut clean, a voice anticipating the next section or clearing early.
5. **Returns real note-lists** that validate to the section length, naming any material downstream
   sections recall.

## PHASE M6 — ASSEMBLE, MEASURE, REFINE (the verification loop)

- **Assemble + validate.** Concatenate in section order, rest-fill tacit voices, build the score,
  confirm every voice validates as note-lists summing to the movement length, and render
  (MusicXML + MIDI; audio where a synth is available).
- **Measure against the design (MEASUREMENT DISCIPLINE).** Confirm the richness landed in the notes:
  the motion curve varies section to section; each accompaniment voice matches its declared role;
  sustained roles are shaped and intentional; moving roles actually move; the reserve is spent where
  designed. Read the numbers as clues, not as musical verdicts.
- **Refine what settled toward the mean.** Where measurement shows a section's accompaniment resting
  on long held values without a declared pad/field/chorale/drone role, either shape it into a real
  sustained field or return those voices to a moving identity, preserving the melodic foreground and
  harmony. Where measurement shows constant motion but the music needs a field, simplify into the
  right sustained accompaniment.

## PHASE M7 — THE ORCHESTRATION-LIFE HUNT (invent broadly across the whole movement)

Across the whole movement, discover and add MANY orchestration-life moments, each section distinct,
so the scoring is full of surprise and individuality:
- staggered and surprising entrances and exits (a voice enters early, a voice clears before the end,
  a voice anticipates the next section across the boundary);
- role swaps and brief cameos (hand the tune or a fragment to an unexpected instrument for a few
  bars, then return it — a harp that takes the melody, a solo low voice, a muted-brass color, a
  timpani motif);
- antiphony and Mahler relay (hand a long line off mid-phrase between families with a 1–2 note
  overlap; trade fragments choir to choir; compress entries toward a declared convergence);
- cross-register rhythmic unison (a triplet or cell doubled across far-apart registers as a color);
- negative space (a harmony cut-out bar of only runs, a grand pause, a voice that drops at the
  destination event then re-enters, the germ passed around before and after its naked statement);
- varied returns (a recap or coda restatement re-orchestrated, re-registered, given a new
  countermelody or relay re-coloring, beyond a transposition).
Treat any example the composer offers as a SEED that shows the KIND of opportunity, and discover
many more of your own; spread them so each section has its own character and no trick repeats.

## PHASE M8 — HANDOFF NOTES (keep the work continuable and the standard legible)

Write a status document beside the movement capturing: where every file is; the governing design law
and the section table; what is done; what is outstanding; the composer's standard in their own words;
and the measurement scripts, so the next pass verifies by measuring and holds the same bar.

## MEASUREMENT DISCIPLINE — confirm complexity is present (read the notes, then the numbers)

Each metric is a positive check that the richness landed:
- **Journey-curve profile** — attack-density per bar, active-voice count, fastest value-class per
  section, and the split peak axes. Confirm the curve varies (sparse valleys against drive crests,
  exposed states, plateaus, arrivals, refusals, or aftermaths) so the form's dynamism is real in the data.
- **Accompaniment role-fit** — per accompaniment voice, compare long-note share, attack density, and
  rhythmic vocabulary to the declared role. A moving figure should move; a pad/field/drone/chorale may
  have high long-note share, but must show intentional register, voicing, entry/release, dynamic shape,
  and harmonic function.
- **Rhythmic vocabulary** — distinct bar-rhythms per voice across a span. Confirm each voice carries
  a varied vocabulary, so the parts read as composed lines.
- **Reserve + event check** — confirm each reserved force sounds exactly where the design spends it,
  and each section's required audible event is present.
Hold these as confirmation of richness; where a number shows a span has settled toward the mean,
return that span to its living identity and measure again.

## EXECUTION — the mechanics (file layout, render, diagnostics)

- **Note-list grammar** — `framework/foundation/score.py`: `(pitch, ql, *marks)`, rest `(None, ql)`,
  chord `(["E-3","G3"], ql)`; flats `"B-"`, sharps `"F#"`; audible dynamics `pp`..`fff`; any unknown
  token renders as a text marking. Bar-sums validate.
- **Frame spine** — `symphony/movements/<mvt>_merged/spine.py` exposes `ROSTER`/`ROSTER_NAMES`,
  `SECTIONS`, `MOTION` (the per-section texture contract), `TEMPO_EVENTS`, `TOTAL`, `chord_map()`,
  the carry-forward cells, and `refs()`.
- **Contract** — `symphony/movements/<mvt>_merged/CONTRACT.md` carries the governing design law (M2)
  the section agents read.
- **Section realization** — `symphony/movements/<mvt>_merged/s<n>_<label>.py` exposes `SECTION_LABEL`
  and `PARTS` (`{roster_name: notelist}`, active voices only; the assembler rest-fills the tacit
  ones), and a `__main__` block asserting every part sums to the section length.
- **Assembler + render** — `symphony/movements/<mvt>_merged/assemble.py` imports the spine + sections,
  rest-fills to the full roster, concatenates in `SECTIONS` order, builds via `mk_part`/`mk_score`,
  validates, and emits MusicXML + MIDI to `outputs/movements/<mvt>_merged/`. Run everything with
  `PYTHONPATH=.`.
- **Diagnostics** — keep the motion-curve and accompaniment-vitality measurements as small scripts run
  against the assembled sections, so each pass confirms the design held.
- **Status** — `symphony/movements/<mvt>_merged/STATUS.md` (Phase M8) keeps the work continuable.
